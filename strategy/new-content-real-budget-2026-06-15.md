## 2. The hard truth

**At 1.5x you must spend R21,739/day to net R5k/day** — and R43,478/day to net R10k. That is R650k–R1.3M of ad spend a month to clear R150k–R300k profit. You're floating a fortune on the card to make a salary, and one bad week of ROAS slippage wipes the whole month.

**At 1.3x you are running a charity.** R0.046 profit per R1 spent. To net R5k/day you'd have to push R108,696/day through Meta — R3.3M/month spend for R150k profit. That is not a business, it's a money-laundering machine that happens to lose a little. Any dip below break-even and you're paying Meta to take your money.

**Break-even ROAS is 1.25x.** Your trailing-7d is 1.55x. You are operating roughly 24% above the line where you start losing money on every rand. That is too thin.

## 3. The only realistic path

Sane spend (R6–12k/day) only produces R5–10k/day profit once ROAS sits at **2.0–2.5x**:

- At **2.0x**: R7,246/day spend → R5k profit. R14,493/day → R10k profit.
- At **2.5x**: R4,348/day spend → R5k profit. R8,696/day → R10k profit.

That jump from 1.5x to 2.0x+ is the entire job of the new campaign. It comes from two levers, not from touching the old account:

1. **New content** — fresh hooks, new faces, new video/image angles that lift CTR and lower CPA (more output from R1 of spend).
2. **Plugging the 61.8% checkout leak** — at AOV R526, recovering even a third of abandoned carts is a direct ROAS multiplier with zero extra ad spend. This is the cheapest ROAS gain available to you.

Frame the campaign goal as: **beat 1.5x → hold 2.0x+.** Not "fix the old ads."

## 4. Recommended 6-ad-set ABO start (at 1.5x reality)

Start small enough that 1.5x can't hurt you, big enough to read signal across 6 sets:

| Setting | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Per ad-set budget | **R150/day** |
| Total daily | **R900/day** (6 × R150) |
| Expected income @1.5x | ~R1,350/day |
| Expected profit @1.5x | **~R207/day** (test stays cash-positive) |
| Break-even floor | **R1.25 ROAS** — below this every set loses money |

Why R150/set: enough daily budget for Meta to exit the learning phase on a R526 AOV product within ~7 days per set, while total spend (R900/day ≈ R27k/month) keeps you near cash-neutral even if ROAS stays at your real 1.5x. You're paying for *data*, not for *profit*, in week one.

Scaling rule: only raise a set's budget once its own ROAS clears **1.8x over 3+ days**. Kill any set under **1.25x**. Do not lift total spend toward the R7k–R12k range until blended ROAS is proven ≥2.0x — at 1.5x, more spend just means more rands earning 23c each.

# International Course/Mentorship Ad Research → DropStore SA Playbook

## What the bigger market is actually doing (2025-2026, post-Andromeda)

**The structural shifts that matter for DropStore:**

1. **Volume over perfection.** Top spenders run ~1 new creative per $3,000/mo spend, testing 40-50 hook variants weekly expecting only 3-5 to win. Meta's Andromeda algorithm penalizes redundancy, so it rewards angle diversity, not 3-4 polished ads. This directly validates Josh's instinct: pump MANY concepts per ad set, not 3-4.
2. **UGC/raw beats polished.** UGC-style ads get ~4.2x engagement and ~2.8x conversion vs polished brand video. Raw iPhone authenticity is now the winning aesthetic, not high-production.
3. **Statics carry TOF, video carries MOF/BOF.** A common split is 60-70% statics / 30-40% video. Statics drive 60-70% of conversions for many accounts. DropStore is over-indexed on video; statics are an under-exploited lever.
4. **AI-UGC is mainstream but regulated.** Arcads (talking-head UGC) and HeyGen are standard for cheap hook-variant volume. WARNING: New York's AI-performer disclosure law starts June 2026 ($1k-5k/violation) — for SA this is low-risk, but if DropStore ever targets US audiences, faceless AI-UGC (Virvid-style) is the safer route. For SA, real student faces still out-convert AI.
5. **Info-product funnels need longer nurture.** High-ticket coaching funnels run 2-6 weeks cold→consult. DropStore's Elite (R999 we-build-it) is the high-ticket tier and should get its own MOF/BOF nurture, separate from Starter/Growth.

## TOF — Top of Funnel (cold, problem/curiosity-led, NO hard sell)

Goal: stop the scroll, plant the idea "an online store is something *I* could actually start." Avoid discounts and price at TOF — they spike CPM. Brand-new people/faces/hooks only.

**Concrete angles to shoot (each is a fresh concept, not a reskin):**

1. **"I tried running an online store for 30 days" diary** — a new face documents day 1 (scared, no idea) → first sale notification → day 30 total. The "I tried X for 30 days" format is a proven curiosity engine. End on the real number, no hype.
2. **Street interview / man-on-the-street** — "What would you do with an extra R5,000 a month?" → cut to the answer → "She started an online store." Cheap to shoot in a CT/Joburg mall, hyper-local, novel for SA feed.
3. **Phone-screen POV first-sale** — silent open on a phone, a Shopify "Cha-ching" sale notification pops, hand reaction. Pattern-interrupt visual, no talking head needed. Reusable as a static too.
4. **Confession hook** — "Nobody tells you this about making money online…" then the honest take (it's not passive, but it's learnable). Confession + contrast positions DropStore as the honest one in a scammy category — a huge SA trust differentiator.
5. **"Day in the life" of a normal SA person** — not a Lambo, just someone packing nothing (digital), checking sales on a couch, school-run, etc. Relatability beats aspiration in SA where "guru" content triggers scam radar.
6. **Qualifying hook** — "If you're a SA student / mom / 9-5er tired of one income, watch this." Self-selects the audience; Meta loves the engagement signal.
7. **Whiteboard / screen-record explainer** — 45-sec "here's how an online store actually makes money" drawn out simply. Demystifies dropshipping for total beginners (the #1 SA objection: "I don't understand how it works").
8. **Cost-breakdown hook** — "Here's exactly what it costs to start an online store in SA (it's less than you think)." Money-specific hooks ("add R3k profit in 30 days" style) convert; localize the rand figure.
9. **Counter-narrative / "stop doing X"** — "Stop applying for jobs. Do this instead." Bold-claim/contrast pattern interrupt.
10. **AI-UGC volume layer** — 10-15 Arcads talking-head variants of the SAME script with different faces/openers, purely to find the winning hook cheaply, then re-shoot the winner with a real person.

## MOF — Middle of Funnel (engaged, objection-handling + proof + education)

Goal: convert "interested" into "trusting." Address the SA-specific objections (is this a scam? do I need money/tech skills? does it work *here*?). 30-60s video is the workhorse here.

**Concrete angles:**

1. **Student interview testimonials** (the ONE area reuse is encouraged) — real SA students, raw iPhone, structured 60% problem / 40% result. Lead with the relatable starting point ("I was a waitress in PE…"), not the outcome. Documentary-style cuts of 3-4 students in one ad also works.
2. **Screen-record proof** — actual Shopify dashboard / sales over time / the AI Store Builder generating a store live. "Show, don't tell" kills the scam objection better than any claim.
3. **Objection-handler series** — one ad per objection: "Do I need money to start?" / "I'm not techy" / "Does dropshipping still work in 2026?" / "Is this a scam?" Founder (Josh) answers each directly to camera, 60-90s. Founder stories build trust before any pitch.
4. **Before/after split-screen** — student's life/bank/confidence before vs after, or store-before (blank) vs store-after (built by the AI tool in minutes). Split-screen before/after is a top MOF format.
5. **Comparison ad** — "Course X charges R10k and gives you PDFs. Here's what R299 gets you + an AI that builds the store for you." Honest competitor comparison is a proven info-product angle. Differentiates the AI Store Builder as the moat.
6. **"I was wrong about dropshipping"** — skeptic-turned-believer arc. The "I was wrong / I almost quit" angle is one of the highest-trust formats.
7. **TikTok-comment-skeptic response** — overlay a real negative/doubtful comment ("this is a scam lol"), then answer it on camera with proof. Turns objection into content.
8. **Behind-the-scenes of the product** — show the actual course modules, the community, the AI builder UI. Demystifies what they're buying and reduces "what do I actually get?" friction.

## BOF — Bottom of Funnel (warm, direct offer + urgency + risk reversal)

Goal: make them act now. Direct, zero ambiguity on what they get, what it costs, and why now. This is where discounts/urgency live.

**Concrete angles:**

1. **Offer-stack static + video** — lay out exactly what's inside each tier (Starter R299 / Growth R449 / Elite R999), what the AI Store Builder does, what the course covers. Text-heavy "mini landing page" statics convert hard at BOF.
2. **Risk-reversal / guarantee** — lead with the guarantee ("build your first store this weekend or…"). Specific guarantee messaging is a top BOF lever; reduces the R299 risk to near-zero in a skeptical market.
3. **Founder direct-offer** — Josh to camera: "Here's everything you get, here's the price, here's why I built this." Founder-led BOF outperforms faceless promo.
4. **Urgency with a real reason** — price-rises, cohort-close, bonus-expiry. Avoid fake countdowns (SA audiences are scam-sensitive); use a real mechanic (e.g., "Elite build slots this month are limited" — true, since you build them).
5. **Reviews/social-proof wall** — rapid-fire student wins, ratings, screenshots of the community. Social proof at BOF closes fence-sitters.
6. **Abandonment-specific BOF** — for the 61.8% checkout abandoners: an ad that names the hesitation ("Still thinking about it? Here's what happened to the last person who waited"). Retargeting-only.
7. **Tier-laddering ad** — push Starter R299 as the no-brainer entry ("less than a night out"), positioning Elite as the upgrade. Tripwire-style low entry, upsell later, matches the proven course-funnel mechanic.

## Offer-mechanic ideas worth stealing from the big players

- **Free webinar / free mini-training as a lead magnet** before the paid course — the dominant info-product TOF→MOF bridge. DropStore could run a "free 20-min: how to start an online store in SA" reg-ad as a cheaper top-funnel entry than selling the course cold.
- **Free test build** (already in DropStore's model) — lean into it HARD as the lead magnet: "Build a real store for free, only pay when you want to launch." This is a stronger hook than any competitor's PDF.
- **Skool/community as a value-add** — courses bundle a community valued separately ($47/mo framing) to justify price. Frame DropStore's community/support as a stacked bonus.
- **Tripwire entry** — Starter R299 as the low-risk tripwire, then upsell Growth/Elite in email + retargeting.

## Cross-cutting rules to bake into every concept

- **New faces, new hooks, new b-roll** per concept — Andromeda penalizes sameness; diversity is the scaling lever.
- **First 3 seconds decide everything** — 73% of video ads fail in the first 3s. Hook must be visual/curiosity/pattern-interrupt, never a logo or "Hi guys."
- **Raw > polished** — shoot on iPhone, vertical, captions burned in.
- **Localize the money** — rand figures, SA accents, SA locations, SA pain (load-shedding-proof income, one-salary households, youth unemployment). This is DropStore's unfair advantage vs the US gurus.
- **Honesty as positioning** — the category is scam-saturated in SA; "the honest one" (confession/comparison/"I was wrong" angles) is the strongest durable wedge.
- **Budget split** ~60/25/15 or 50/30/20 TOF/MOF/BOF; keep ROAS math at 1.3-1.5x for budgeting (profit/day = spend × (0.92×ROAS − 1.15)).
