## v2 refinements — Andromeda 2.0 (Meta Performance Summit KB, 2026-06-15)
- **Fewer, more diverse ads BEATS volume.** The signal-journey system serves the right ad to each user in the right order; flooding it with near-dupes makes that decision HARDER. Confirms the rotation-pool — keep Wave 1 lean, do NOT dump all 43/14 at once.
- **Carve ~10% to brand/relationship, not 100% conversion.** Panel was blunt: "no brand = it won't work." → ~R150/day to creator/partnership content that builds the brand, not direct-sale.
- **Creators are the moat → Partnership Ads.** Turn real students into affiliate creators; run them through Meta's Partnership Ads Hub. Brief with bullet points, NOT a full script — over-scripting kills authenticity and the creator's audience trust. This is video angle #10 (real member) scaled into a system.
- **Enable Conversions API one-click install** (Business Manager) — match rate jumps (low 7s → high 8s) = better signal + optimisation. Quick technical win; needs the base pixel already on site.
- **Define the real success event** — add a "new customer" signal/event, not just generic purchase, so Meta optimises for acquisition (not repeat buyers). Ties to last night's "is it incremental?" question.
- **Run a Conversion Lift Study** (free, Ads Manager → Experiments). Settles the pixel-vs-real-income gap the teardown flagged (scale-up incremental was ~0.1x). Decide budget off causal data, not attributed ROAS.
- **10% R&D budget** for weird tests / new formats (Moment Maker, trending-ad). Lives inside Testing CBO.

**Budget re-cut (R1,500/day):** Scaling ~R950 (63%) · Testing ~R400 (27%, incl. R&D) · Brand/creator partnership ~R150 (10%).

## v3 refinement — ABO vs CBO (Levi Steede KB, transcribed 2026-06-15)
Levi's decision rule maps cleanly to DropStore (established brand, known hero at 5.92x, knows its personas):
- **SCALE via ABO, not CBO.** Use ABO when you can afford ≥2x target CPA per ad set AND you're confident in the concept — it scales known winners *harder* and gives clean per-concept control. DropStore qualifies, and it's literally the structure already running ("D | ABO | Warm Up" at 1.65x). So: keep ABO for scaling — 1 concentrated ad set on the **AI System hero**, budget ≥2x CPA (~R650/day min). Feed it dimensional variations.
- **TEST via CBO.** Use CBO when hit-rate on the new batch is uncertain (it is — these are unproven) — Meta filters the losers for you and protects margin. Load the testing CBO ad set with **20–30 genuinely diverse creatives** (Andromeda wants this); Meta self-distributes to 1–3, spends pennies on the rest, so it won't waste budget. **No minimum-spend per ad set** (defeats CBO) — except optionally 10% on one creative you want to force-test.
- This REVERSES the earlier "two CBOs": correct shape for DropStore = **Scaling ABO (hero) + Testing CBO (diverse batch) + small brand/creator slice.**
- ABO setup: 4–8 variations within the concept, run 3–5 days, scale or kill on CPA. Switch a CBO→ABO once spend spreads evenly across many creatives and Meta is finding winners consistently (sign you can scale aggressively).
- Net effect on the creative plan: the Testing CBO can hold MORE of the 43 statics + 14 videos at once than the conservative 11 — load ~20–30 diverse, let Meta pick. The bench still exists for rotation as winners fatigue.
