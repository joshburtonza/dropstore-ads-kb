## Executive summary

# Executive Summary: The DropStore Scale Playbook

**The goal in one line:** Get from ~R2.5k/day spend and ~R5k/day revenue to a sustainable **R7k profit/day floor**, with R10k/day as the stretch ceiling. Not "go hard." Go hard *without dying.*

---

## The Math (read this first)

We sell a course plus a done-for-you AI store build at ~95% margin (Starter R299, Growth R449, Elite R999, plus the R60 "skip the line" upsell). Breakeven is roughly **1.2x MER** once you load the 15% VAT on ad spend. We currently run ~1.7x blended MER. December proved ~2.1x at R9k/day spend.

| Lever | Floor (commit) | Stretch (chase) |
|---|---|---|
| Daily ad spend | R12k | R20-22k |
| Daily store revenue | R20-22k | R30-37k |
| Blended MER | >= 1.6x | 1.7-2.1x |
| **Daily profit** | **R7k** | **R10k** |

Judge everything on **blended MER (Shopify revenue / total spend)**, never pixel ROAS. The pixel is half-blind on mobile in-app browsers and totally blind to Sophia's WhatsApp closes. That single rule keeps you from killing winners that look dead on the dashboard.

---

## Campaign Structure at a Glance

Start **consolidated, not fragmented.** One broad Advantage+ prospecting campaign plus one retargeting ad set. Meta's 2026 delivery (Andromeda) rewards budget liquidity and punishes the old 6-ad-set tree by starving each set below the ~50-conversions/week it needs to learn. Keep tight ABO testing only as a controlled side lane, never as the default. **Always route paid to the homepage** (all 3 tiers, ~96% of orders close there), never a single product page.

The buying reality we build around: South African, mobile-first (88%), Monday to Wednesday strongest, **19:00-22:00 the kill window.**

---

## The Five Things That Actually Matter

1. **Creative supply is the real ceiling, not budget.** At R12-22k/day you burn through 15-25 fresh ads a week (creatives fatigue at ~R700-1.5k spend). No factory, no scale. This needs a named owner, a weekly quota board, a student-win capture flow with a signed release built in, and a contracted editor on 24-48h turnaround. Build it *before* spend passes R5k/day.
2. **Fix the 97% leak before you ladder.** Sessions-to-cart sits at ~3%. Taking that to 6% halves your effective CAC permanently, worth more than any creative win. Week one is for the page and CAPI at *flat* spend, then climb.
3. **Get attribution that can rank ad sets.** Blended MER is a store-level number; it cannot tell you which ad to scale. Get CAPI plus the WhatsApp purchase-fire live (EMQ 7+) so the *ranking* between ad sets becomes trustworthy. Without it, "cut the laggard" is guessing.
4. **Stay on the safe angle, but build a bench.** "AI builds your whole store" is the proven, compliant winner. Lean on it. But it *will* fatigue across a finite SA pool, so develop 2-3 new compliant angles now (the setup-quit problem, speed/comparison, learn-how-stores-work) while the hero still works.
5. **Fund the float before you need it.** Daily payments to Meta plus VAT, against payouts that lag 1-2 days, plus a likely Yoco rolling reserve. Hold **5-7 days of peak spend (R140-180k) in working capital** before the top rungs, or a profitable business pauses winning ad sets mid-scale and resets Meta's learning. That cash crunch, not the ad account, is the most likely way this plan dies.

---

## The Single Biggest Risk

**The Meta ad account is already flagged, and the goal itself is the trap.** "Make R10k a day" is exactly the income-claim language that gets money-opportunity accounts banned. Every creative stays on the *capability* ("AI builds your store"), never the *outcome* ("get rich"). One reviewer, one competitor mass-report, or one refund spike can disable the account, and at R20k/day a 3-day appeal is R60k of stranded momentum plus a dead pixel mid-learning.

So we plan to **survive** the ban, not just avoid it. The resolution to our own playbook's internal disagreement: **keep one live account** for daily ops (a parallel account to dodge the flag *is* ban evasion, do not do it). Separately, warm a backup Business Manager under a genuinely distinct legal identity, page, pixel and payment rail at R200/day starting now, as cold insurance. Write the ban-day runbook (who appeals, what it says, how revenue reroutes inside 24h, what Sophia and organic carry while paid is dark). **Do not scale past R6k/day until that runbook exists and the backup has 7+ clean days.**

---

## First-Week Move

Do not touch the top of the budget. Week one:

1. **Hold spend flat at ~R3-5k/day.** Fix the homepage leak and ship CAPI plus the WhatsApp purchase-fire. This is the foundation everything else multiplies on.
2. **Stand up the creative factory.** Batch-film 8-10 founder hooks in one Henro and Seth sitting, turn on the one-tap student-win submission flow with the release baked in, lock the editor.
3. **Start the backup BM warming at R200/day** and start the ban-day runbook.
4. **Set the scorecard, then ladder.** Floor: R5-7k profit/day at R12-15k spend, MER >= 1.6x, no Meta strikes, refunds <5%, 15+ creatives/week. Stop conditions: MER <1.4x for 3 days = hold; any Meta strike = freeze; chargebacks >0.7% = slow; cash buffer <3 days peak spend = stop laddering.

Fix the leak, the float, the creative pipe, the attribution and the ban plan first. Then, and only then, climb the budget. Commit to R7k/day. Chase R10k.

*No em dashes anywhere in customer-facing creative. The voice is South African, founder-to-builder, short and real.*

## Campaign Architecture

## Campaign Architecture

This is the structural backbone of the DropStore Meta account. Everything downstream (creative, copy, scaling rules) plugs into this tree. Built for a R12k to R22k/day aggressive budget targeting R10k profit/day, sized to DropStore's real economics (~95% margin, breakeven ROAS ~1.16 to 1.2x, avg order ~R450, ZAR, 88% mobile).

### 0. The account decision: keep the pixel and account, archive the campaigns

**Do NOT spin up a new ad account or pixel. Archive the old campaigns and rebuild inside the existing account.** Reasons, specific to DropStore:

- **The pixel holds your conversion history.** Your pixel has been firing CompleteRegistration / Purchase / AddToCart events through the December R9k/day run. That signal is what lets Meta find buyers fast. A fresh pixel starts blind and you eat a 2 to 4 week relearning tax at exactly the moment you want to scale. You cannot afford a cold pixel at R12k+/day.
- **A new ad account does not un-flag you.** The account is already flagged (read-only for automated changes). Opening a second account on the same business / payment method / domain is detectable and is treated by Meta as ban evasion. That is the fastest way to lose the Business Manager entirely, not just one account. Rehabilitate the account you have.
- **What actually de-risks the flag is creative, not a new account.** The flag risk is driven by income-claim "make R10k" creative on a money-opportunity vertical. The fix is changing what you say, not where you say it (see audience and creative rules below). Get the account back to active-changes status first by submitting a review request and pulling all hard income-claim creative, then rebuild.

**Pre-launch account hygiene (do before any new campaign goes live):**
- Archive (do not delete) all legacy campaigns so historical data stays queryable for reporting.
- Confirm Domain Verification on the Shopify store domain in Business Settings.
- Set Aggregated Event Measurement priority: Purchase #1, InitiateCheckout #2, AddToCart #3, ViewContent #4, Lead/CompleteReg #5.
- **Set up the Conversions API (CAPI).** This is non-negotiable at this budget. In-app browsers (Instagram/Facebook) strip referrer to "direct" and the browser pixel is blind to your WhatsApp/Sophia closes. CAPI via Shopify's native Meta channel (server-side) plus the pixel recovers a large chunk of the events you are currently missing. Without CAPI, Meta optimises on a fraction of your real conversions and ROAS looks fake-low.

### 1. The full tree to launch

Three campaigns, mapped to funnel temperature. All Sales (Conversion) objective, all optimising for **Purchase** (you have the volume to optimise on Purchase, not AddToCart, especially with CAPI feeding it). All driving to the **homepage** (the 3-tier page that converts ~96% of orders), never a single product page.

```
DS | Sales | TOF | Prospecting        (CBO is tempting here; default ABO, see below)
├── adset: TOF-Broad-A          R3,500/day
├── adset: TOF-Broad-B          R3,500/day
└── adset: TOF-Interest-AIBiz   R2,000/day
        each adset: 4 ads  (3 VIDEO + 1 IMAGE)

DS | Sales | MOF | Retarget-Warm       (ABO)
├── adset: MOF-VV+Engagers-7d    R1,800/day
└── adset: MOF-VV+Engagers-30d   R1,200/day
        each adset: 3 ads  (2 VIDEO + 1 IMAGE)

DS | Sales | BOF | Retarget-Hot        (ABO)
└── adset: BOF-ATC+IC-14d        R2,000/day
        adset: 3 ads  (1 VIDEO + 2 IMAGE/offer)
```

**Counts at a glance (mid-budget ~R17.5k/day baseline):**

| Layer | Campaigns | Adsets | Ads/adset | Video/adset | Image/adset | Total live ads |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| TOF | 1 | 3 | 4 | 3 | 1 | 12 |
| MOF | 1 | 2 | 3 | 2 | 1 | 6 |
| BOF | 1 | 1 | 3 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
| **Total** | **3** | **6** | — | — | — | **21** |

Why these counts: at R12 to 22k/day you generate enough conversion volume to feed roughly 6 adsets without fragmenting the optimiser. More adsets than this at this budget starves each one below the ~50 weekly purchase threshold and slows learning. TOF gets the most ads because that is where fatigue is fastest and where you need horizontal creative testing. BOF gets fewest because the audience is tiny.

**Video vs image split, and why:** TOF leans video (3:1) because your proven winners ("First AI System", "Should be asking abt AI") are video/UGC and video carries the "AI builds your whole store" demo better. BOF leans image/offer (2:1) because hot audiences do not need convincing, they need a nudge (the DROP10 offer, the 3-tier price card, a "still thinking?" static). MOF sits in the middle (2:1 video).

### 2. Budget: ABO by default, CBO only as a later test

**Default to ABO (Ad-set Budget Optimisation) for control.** You got burned by a CBO at 0.47x. CBO hands Meta the allocation decision, and on a flagged account with a leaky funnel that produced a blowout. ABO lets you set the rand per adset and protect MOF/BOF spend from being cannibalised by a hungry TOF adset. You keep the steering wheel.

CBO is not banned forever. Once you have 2 weeks of clean ABO data and 2+ proven winner creatives, you may run ONE CBO test campaign capped at ~15% of daily spend (~R2.5k) to compare. Never put your whole budget on CBO again.

**Rand/day per adset and the funnel split (shown at low / mid / high budget):**

| Adset | % of spend | R12k/day | R17.5k/day | R22k/day |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TOF-Broad-A | 20% | R2,400 | R3,500 | R4,400 |
| TOF-Broad-B | 20% | R2,400 | R3,500 | R4,400 |
| TOF-Interest-AIBiz | 11% | R1,300 | R2,000 | R2,400 |
| MOF-VV+Eng-7d | 10% | R1,200 | R1,800 | R2,200 |
| MOF-VV+Eng-30d | 7% | R850 | R1,200 | R1,550 |
| BOF-ATC+IC-14d | 12% | R1,450 | R2,000 | R2,650 |
| **TOF total** | **~51%** | **R6,100** | **R9,000** | **R11,200** |
| **MOF total** | **~17%** | **R2,050** | **R3,000** | **R3,750** |
| **BOF total** | **~12%** | **R1,450** | **R2,000** | **R2,650** |

Top-of-funnel takes the majority (~50 to 55%) because your retargeting pools are small relative to a R20k/day cold spend and will saturate fast if overfunded. The retargeting layers punch above their spend (warm/hot audiences carry your best ROAS) so they get a smaller rand figure but a high frequency cap, not a high budget.

**Important scaling note:** scale by raising the daily budget on winning ABO adsets ~20% every 48 to 72h, NOT by jumping straight to R22k on day 1. Cold-launch at the R12k floor, climb as ROAS holds and as Yoco capacity allows. Which brings up the hard constraint below.

**Cash-collection gate (Yoco):** Yoco recently froze new online-payment activations. Before you push revenue toward R25 to 37k/day, confirm your current Yoco account can actually settle that volume without holds. Scaling ad spend faster than your processor can collect just builds a receivables backlog and a support fire. Treat processor capacity as a real ceiling on the budget ramp, and have a backup rail (Payfast/Peach) ready if Yoco caps you.

### 3. Naming convention

One scheme, top to bottom, pipe-delimited so reporting filters cleanly and you can read temperature/offer at a glance in the dashboard.

**Campaign:** `DS | {Objective} | {Funnel} | {Theme}`
- `DS | Sales | TOF | Prospecting`
- `DS | Sales | MOF | Retarget-Warm`
- `DS | Sales | BOF | Retarget-Hot`

**Adset:** `{Funnel}-{Audience}-{Window} | {Budget}`
- `TOF-Broad-A | R3500`
- `TOF-Interest-AIBiz | R2000`
- `MOF-VV+Eng-7d | R1800`
- `BOF-ATC+IC-14d | R2000`

**Ad:** `{Format}-{Angle}-{Hook}-{v##}-{YYMMDD}`
- `VID-AIbuild-FirstAISystem-v03-260613`
- `VID-StudentWin-Henro-v01-260613`
- `IMG-Offer-DROP10-3tier-v02-260613`
- `IMG-PriceCard-Growth449-v01-260613`

Rules that keep reporting clean:
- Format prefix is always `VID` or `IMG` so you can pivot spend by format in one filter.
- Angle is a fixed vocabulary: `AIbuild`, `StudentWin`, `Founder`, `Offer`, `Objection`, `PriceCard`. Do not invent new angle tags ad hoc.
- Version number increments per iteration of the same concept; date stamp is launch date so you can age creatives and trigger refresh.
- Never reuse a name. A retired creative keeps its name in the archive; a new cut gets a new `v##`.

### 4. Audience definitions per layer

**TOF (cold, broad-first):**
- `TOF-Broad-A` and `TOF-Broad-B`: **fully broad.** Location South Africa, age 18 to 45, all genders, Advantage+ audience / no detailed targeting. Two identical broad adsets so you have two independent learning lanes and can kill one without resetting the other. Broad wins on SA dropshipping because Meta's algorithm finds the side-hustle / income-curious buyer better than manual interests do, and broad keeps you off the narrow "make money" signals that compound flag risk.
- `TOF-Interest-AIBiz`: SA, 18 to 45, layered interests around the proven angle, not the income angle: Shopify, Dropshipping, Online business, AliExpress, Entrepreneurship, ChatGPT / Artificial intelligence, Side hustle. This adset exists to give Meta a warmer seed and to A/B against pure broad. If broad consistently beats it, fold its budget into Broad-A/B.
- Exclusions on ALL TOF adsets: exclude existing Purchasers (180d), exclude current ATC/IC (14d), exclude your customer-list custom audience. No point paying cold prices to reach people already deeper in the funnel.

**MOF (warm retargeting):**
- `MOF-VV+Eng-7d`: people in the last **7 days** who are (a) video viewers at **50%+** of any DropStore video, OR (b) Instagram engagers, OR (c) Facebook Page engagers. **Exclude** anyone who hit AddToCart or InitiateCheckout (those belong to BOF) and exclude Purchasers 180d. This is the freshest warm pool, highest intent of the warm layer.
- `MOF-VV+Eng-30d`: same composition (50%+ video viewers + IG/FB engagers) over a **30-day** window, excluding the 7-day pool, excluding ATC/IC, excluding Purchasers 180d. Catches the slower-considerers who saw you a few weeks back.
- Why 50%+ video view, not 25%: your funnel leaks hard (97% never add to cart), so retarget only people who actually watched, not idle impressions. 50%+ is the meaningful-attention threshold.

**BOF (hot retargeting):**
- `BOF-ATC+IC-14d`: AddToCart OR InitiateCheckout in the last **14 days**, **excluding Purchasers (14d)** so you stop paying to chase someone who already bought. 14 days because avg order is small (~R450), the decision cycle is short, and the pool is tiny. This is where the DROP10 offer and the 3-tier price-card statics live. These people already showed buying intent and many of the "lost" ones are actually closing via Sophia on WhatsApp, so keep BOF spend modest and let it do the nudge job.
- Optional second BOF adset only once the ATC pool is large enough (>1,000): `BOF-ViewContent-7d` excluding ATC/IC and Purchasers, to catch product-page bouncers. Do not launch this on day 0; the pool is too thin.

A note tying audiences back to the flag risk: keep the **targeting** clean of income-opportunity signals (lean broad + AI/Shopify interests, not "get rich" interests) AND keep the **creative** off hard income claims. The combination of a money-opportunity audience plus "make R10k" creative is the exact pattern Meta restricts. The AI-build angle is both your best performer and your safest framing, so it does double duty.

### 5. Creative volume target and refresh / kill cadence

**The bottleneck is creative supply, not budget.** Fatigue ceiling is ~R700 to R1,500 spend per creative. At R12 to 22k/day you burn through that on roughly 15 to 25 fresh ads per week. Plan production around that number or the account stalls.

**Weekly creative target: 15 to 20 net-new ads/week minimum (aim 20 at the R22k end).**
- ~12 to 14 VIDEO/week (UGC student-wins, founder-led Henro & Seth, AI-build screen-demo). Video is the engine; it must dominate the pipeline.
- ~4 to 6 IMAGE/week (3-tier price cards, DROP10 offer statics, objection/FAQ statics for BOF).
- Run a winner-iteration loop: every proven concept (e.g. "First AI System") gets 2 to 3 new hooks/intros per week, since the back half of a winning video is reusable and only the first 3 seconds fatigue.

**Verdict windows and kill rules (judge on BLENDED MER, Shopify revenue / spend, NOT pixel ROAS, because the pixel undercounts in-app and WhatsApp sales):**

| Stage | Window | Spend gate | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| New ad first look | 24h | ~R300 to 400 spent | If 0 ATC and CTR < ~0.8%, pause. Do not let a dud bleed. |
| Early verdict | 48 to 72h | ~R700 to 1,000 spent | Below breakeven blended (~1.2x) with no purchases → kill. At or above → keep, consider a small budget bump. |
| Fatigue check | rolling | per-creative lifetime | Once a creative crosses ~R1,000 to 1,500 lifetime spend AND frequency > ~2.5 (MOF/BOF) or CPA climbs >25% off its 7-day best → swap it out, even if it was a past winner. |
| Winner scale | 48 to 72h cadence | — | Raise the host adset budget ~20% while blended MER holds. Never double overnight (resets learning). |

Operational rhythm:
- **Refresh TOF creative weekly.** Assume every TOF ad has a ~7 to 10 day useful life at this spend.
- **Refresh MOF/BOF every 10 to 14 days** (smaller audiences fatigue on frequency, so watch frequency more than spend there).
- Keep a bench of 5+ ready-to-launch ads at all times so a kill is instantly backfilled, never leaving an adset thin.
- Honour the buying-window reality: Monday to Wednesday and 19:00 to 22:00 SAST are peak. Launch fresh creative early in the week (Mon AM) so it learns before the Mon to Wed buying peak rather than wasting its learning phase on a slow weekend.

### 6. Launch checklist: day 0 to week 2

**Day 0 (setup, before spend):**
1. Submit account review request and pull/disable all hard income-claim ("make R10k", "guaranteed income") creative still attached anywhere.
2. Archive all legacy campaigns (do not delete).
3. Confirm domain verification + AEM event priority (Purchase #1).
4. Stand up CAPI via Shopify Meta channel; confirm server events arriving in Events Manager (target event match quality "Good" or better).
5. Build the 4 custom/retargeting audiences: VV50%+7d, VV50%+30d, IG/FB engagers, ATC+IC 14d, plus Purchasers-180d and customer-list for exclusions.
6. Confirm Yoco (or backup processor) can handle the projected revenue ramp without activation holds.
7. Build the 3 campaigns / 6 adsets / 21 ads per the tree, ABO, all to homepage, optimise for Purchase.
8. Load opening creative: 12 TOF ads (9 vid / 3 img), 6 MOF (4 vid / 2 img), 3 BOF (1 vid / 2 img). Confirm DROP10 popup is live on site.

**Day 0 to 3 (cold start at the floor):**
- Launch at the **R12k/day floor**, not R22k. Let it learn.
- Do not touch anything for the first 48h beyond killing obvious duds (0 ATC + CTR <0.8% at ~R350 spend).
- Watch blended MER daily against the ~1.2x breakeven. Confirm CAPI events flowing.

**Day 3 to 7 (first verdicts):**
- Apply the 48 to 72h verdict to every ad. Kill below-breakeven, keep winners.
- Backfill killed ads from the bench (maintain full ad counts).
- If blended MER is holding above ~1.5x, begin 20% budget bumps on winning adsets every 48 to 72h, climbing toward R15 to 17k/day.
- Confirm one TOF adset (broad vs interest) is clearly leading; reallocate from the laggard.

**Week 2 (scale + systematise):**
- Push toward R18 to 22k/day only if blended MER holds ≥1.7x and Yoco is settling cleanly. December proved ~2.1x is achievable at R9k/day, so the target band is 1.7 to 2.1x at the higher spend.
- Confirm the creative pipeline is delivering 15 to 20 net-new ads/week; if it is not, that is your hard ceiling and you cap spend there rather than re-running fatigued ads.
- Begin the single capped CBO test (~R2.5k, ~15% of spend) against ABO only now, with 2+ proven winners to feed it.
- Stand up a daily reporting view keyed on the naming convention (filter by VID/IMG, by funnel layer, by angle) and review every morning against blended MER, not pixel ROAS.
- Track the WhatsApp/Sophia close rate separately and credit it back to the layer that sourced the lead, since the pixel cannot see those sales and they materially change which ads look profitable.

## Andromeda / Meta 2026 Delivery Mechanics

## Andromeda / Meta 2026 Delivery Mechanics

This is the part most South African dropshippers get wrong. They still run ads like it's 2021: pick an interest, stack three more on top, split into ten little adsets, and babysit each ad. That whole playbook is dead. Meta's new delivery engine (Andromeda, the retrieval layer that sits under Advantage+) changed what actually wins. Read this section once, then run everything else in the playbook through this lens.

### What the 2026 system actually rewards

Andromeda is the model that decides, in milliseconds, which of your ads to even consider showing to a person before the auction. It rebuilt how Meta retrieves and ranks creative. In plain terms, here is what it now rewards:

1. **Creative diversity and volume.** The system wants lots of different ads so it can match the right message to the right person. One hero ad and two backups is starvation. It wants 6 to 12+ distinct creatives live per campaign so it has options to test against millions of micro-audiences.
2. **Broad targeting.** Andromeda is better at finding your buyer than you are at describing them. Broad (age + country + gender, or full Advantage+) consistently beats hand-picked interests now, because the machine reads the creative and the pixel signal instead of your guesses.
3. **Budget liquidity.** Money needs room to flow to whatever is working right now. Tight, fragmented budgets handcuff the algorithm. Fewer, better-funded adsets let it move spend in real time.
4. **Signal and CAPI.** The whole thing runs on conversion data. The more clean purchase signal you feed back (server-side via the Conversions API, not just the browser pixel), the smarter the matching gets. Weak signal = dumb delivery.
5. **Sequence learning.** Andromeda has gotten good at multi-touch. One ad warms a cold person up, a different ad closes them days later. The system understands this chain. If you judge each ad in isolation you will kill the warmer and wonder why the closer stopped converting.

The mental shift: **you are not a media buyer picking audiences anymore. You are a creative supplier feeding a matching machine, and a signal plumber making sure it can see what works.** For DropStore, where the pixel is half-blind and the cash bottleneck is real, that framing matters more than for most.

### Creative IS the targeting now

This is the single most important line in the playbook. **The creative you make is now the targeting.** Andromeda reads your ad (the hook, the visuals, the words, who's on screen, what problem it names) and uses that to decide who sees it. So you control who you reach by what you say, not by which interest box you tick.

What this means for reaching the right SA buyer for DropStore:

- **Stop interest-stacking.** Do not build an adset targeting "Entrepreneurship + Shopify + Online business + Passive income." That cage just shrinks the pool Andromeda can pull from and tells it nothing it can't already infer. Go broad: South Africa, 18 to 45 (or 18 to 55, test it), all genders, Advantage+ audience on. Let the creative do the sorting.
- **The proven DropStore winner ("AI builds your whole store for you") already targets the right person.** When the ad opens with "an AI built this entire online store in minutes," it self-selects the curious-but-overwhelmed beginner who wants the shortcut. That hook IS your targeting. The "First AI System" and "Should be asking abt AI" creatives that pulled 4 to 7x did the audience-finding through the message, not through a settings panel.
- **Reach different SA buyer types by changing the creative, not the adset.** Want the side-hustle mom in Durban? Make a UGC video of a student-win that looks like her. Want the young guy in Joburg who already tried dropshipping and failed? Founder VSL from Henro or Seth on "why your first store flopped and how the AI fixes it." Same broad adset, different creative, different person reached. That is the whole game now.
- **Speak SA.** Andromeda reads language and tone too. Copy and voiceovers that sound local (real SA phrasing, ZAR pricing on screen, "boet/bru" energy where natural) will get matched to local buyers more reliably than generic American dropshipping-guru tone.

### Budget liquidity, and why too many tiny adsets kills you

Every adset needs enough volume to exit the learning phase, which in practice means roughly **50 conversions in a rolling 7-day window.** DropStore's average order is ~R450, so one purchase per ~R450 to R750 of spend at current efficiency. Do the math: an adset needs real money before the algorithm has enough data to optimise.

If you fragment R2.5k/day across eight adsets, that's ~R310 each. None of them ever learns. They all sit in permanent learning-limited purgatory, delivery is jittery, CPAs swing wildly, and you blame the creative when the real problem is starvation.

The fix is **budget consolidation:**

- Run **one or two campaigns, with one to three adsets total**, not ten. Use CBO / Advantage+ campaign budget so Meta pools the money and pushes it to whatever ad is winning that hour. That's budget liquidity in action.
- Concretely for DropStore right now (~R2.5k/day): **one Advantage+ shopping campaign, broad SA, single budget, 6 to 10 creatives inside it.** That's it. Stop there.
- When you scale toward the R12 to R22k/day needed for R10k profit days, **do NOT scale by adding adsets.** Scale by raising the one campaign budget (no more than ~20 to 30% per day so you don't reset learning) and by adding fresh creative. More money + more creative in the SAME structure. Not more boxes.
- One sane exception: a separate small **retargeting / warm adset** (site visitors, add-to-carts, IG/FB engagers, video viewers) can run alongside the broad prospecting campaign. That's it. Two structures max.

Why this matters extra for DropStore: your December best (2.1x MER at R9k/day) almost certainly came from concentrated spend on a few proven creatives, not from spreading thin. Liquidity is how you get back there.

### Sequence learning: never kill a high-CPA ad inside a winning adset

This is where founders torch their own results. You open the ad-level report, see one ad showing R900 CPA against a R450 order, and you pause it on reflex. Stop.

Here's what's actually happening. Inside a winning adset, your ads are working as a **relay team.** A UGC student-win video catches a cold scroller and plants the "AI builds your store" idea. That person doesn't buy. Three days later a founder VSL or a static price ad re-reaches them and they convert. **Meta's attribution often hands the whole sale to the closer, and the warmer shows an ugly CPA even though it did half the work.** Kill the warmer and the closer's CPA quietly collapses too, because the closer was never the thing that started the chain.

Rules:

- **Judge at the ADSET and CAMPAIGN level, not the ad level.** If the campaign's blended MER is healthy, the campaign is healthy. Leave the ad mix alone. The algorithm is using all of them.
- **Do not pause an individual ad just because its standalone CPA looks bad** when the adset overall is profitable. The only ad-level reason to act is the fatigue signal below (frequency climbing), not raw CPA.
- This is doubly true for DropStore because your pixel undercounts hard. An ad's "bad" CPA in Ads Manager might be hiding WhatsApp closes via Sophia and in-app-browser sales the pixel logged as "direct." The ad you're about to kill might be your best top-of-funnel earner. Blended MER is the judge. Ad-level pixel CPA is noise.

### Frequency: your fatigue early-warning system

You need ONE clean signal for "this ad is burning out," and it's frequency, read correctly:

- **Read it at the AD level** (not adset, not campaign).
- **Use a 14-day window.**
- **On COLD / prospecting only** (warm and retargeting naturally run higher frequency, so the threshold doesn't apply there).
- **Target: keep it under ~2.0.** Under 2 on cold means you're still reaching fresh people. That's healthy.

What to do as it climbs:

- **Under 2.0:** all good, leave it, let it spend.
- **2.0 to 3.0:** yellow light. This ad has shown to the available cold pool a lot. Performance will start sliding. Have the next batch of creative ready to go in.
- **Over 3.0 on cold:** the ad is fatigued. You're paying to re-show it to people who already ignored it twice. **Swap in fresh creative.** Do not just bump the budget (that accelerates burnout) and do not panic-kill the whole adset (sequence learning). Rotate the tired ad out, drop new ones in.

Frequency climbing fast is also your tell that **creative supply is the bottleneck**, which for DropStore it absolutely is. At R12 to R22k/day you'll burn through 15 to 25 fresh ads a week because each creative fatigues around R700 to R1.5k of spend. If frequency is spiking, the answer is almost never "change the targeting." It's "make more ads."

### Format diversity inside the set

Don't make six versions of the same video. Andromeda matches better when it has genuinely different *formats* to choose from, because different humans stop for different things. Inside a single adset, deliberately mix:

- **UGC video** (student-win, talking-to-camera, screen-record of the AI building a store). Catches the scroller who trusts "a real person like me." This is DropStore's bread and butter.
- **Founder VSL** (Henro or Seth, longer, story-led, "here's why I built this"). Closes the considered buyer and carries authority.
- **Static image** (clean, bold, ZAR price + the AI angle, one big claim). Cheap to make, fast to test, and the in-app browser crowd often converts off a simple strong static.
- **Screenshot / "receipt" style** (a real store the AI built, a payout, a DROP10 order confirmation, a WhatsApp chat with Sophia). High trust, low production cost, very SA-native.

Why mix: each format reaches a different slice of the same broad audience and they feed the sequence (a static catches attention, the VSL closes later). It also spreads your fatigue risk so the whole adset doesn't tire at once. And critically for DropStore, **mixed formats let you scale creative volume without scaling production cost** since statics and screenshots are quick to pump out, which directly attacks your creative-supply bottleneck.

### Concrete "feed the algorithm" rules for DropStore

These are specific to your situation. Follow them in order.

1. **Fix the signal before you scale the spend.** Confirm CAPI (Conversions API) is fully live and deduped against the pixel. Your in-app-browser traffic (88% mobile, mostly IG/FB browser) strips referrers and the browser pixel goes blind. Server-side CAPI is the only way Andromeda sees enough purchase signal to optimise. Scaling spend on top of a half-blind pixel just teaches the machine garbage. This is step one, full stop.

2. **Judge on BLENDED MER, never pixel ROAS.** Your north star is Shopify revenue divided by ad spend. Right now ~1.7x, breakeven ~1.16 to 1.2x, December peak 2.1x. The pixel will always look worse than reality because it can't see Sophia's WhatsApp closes or in-app sales. Set the team's dashboard to blended MER and make every scale/cut decision off it.

3. **Send paid traffic to the HOMEPAGE, always.** It shows all three tiers (R299 / R449 / R999) and converts ~96% of orders. Never route a paid ad to a single product page. The homepage plus the DROP10 popup is your conversion engine; ads only need to get the click there.

4. **Stay broad. One campaign, one budget, 6 to 12 creatives.** No interest stacking. Advantage+ shopping, SA, broad age. Let creative target. Add a single warm/retargeting adset on the side, nothing more.

5. **Lean on the proven winner and its angle.** "The AI builds your whole store for you" is your highest-matching message. Build most new creative as fresh variations on that spine (new hook, new face, new format) rather than chasing new angles cold.

6. **Make creative the priority, because it's the real constraint.** Set up a weekly creative factory targeting 15 to 25 fresh ads/week before you push spend past ~R5k/day. Use student wins, founder clips, and quick statics/screenshots. You cannot out-spend a creative shortage; you'll just spike frequency and watch MER fall.

7. **THE EXISTENTIAL CONSTRAINT: do not feed Meta income-claim creative.** The account is already flagged and read-only for automated changes. "Make R10k a day," "quit your job," "guaranteed income," "financial freedom" are exactly the money-opportunity claims that get accounts restricted and banned, and yours is already on thin ice. The whole "go hard to R10k profit/day" plan dies instantly if the account gets banned. Frame everything around the *product and the outcome of using it* ("AI builds you a real online store in minutes," "see the store it built," "no tech skills needed"), not around income promises. This is non-negotiable and it overrides any creative idea that tests well but flirts with earnings claims.

8. **Mind the cash plumbing while you scale.** Yoco froze new online-payment activations, which is a real revenue-collection bottleneck. Going from ~R2.5k to R12 to R22k/day spend means a big jump in orders and cash to collect. Confirm the payment rails can actually take the volume before you open the budget taps, otherwise you'll be paying Meta to generate orders you can't collect on.

9. **Scale on time, not just creative.** Mon to Wed are your strongest sales days and 19:00 to 22:00 SAST is the buying window. When you push budget, push it into the proven days and the evening window first rather than flat-lining spend across a dead Sunday morning. Let the campaign run continuously (Advantage+ prefers it) but expect and allow the budget to breathe toward those peaks.

**The one-line summary for the team:** feed the machine clean signal (CAPI) and a fat, diverse creative pipeline through one broad, well-funded campaign to the homepage, judge everything on blended MER at campaign level, rotate ads when cold frequency passes ~2, never kill a warmer for a bad standalone CPA, and never, ever run income-claim copy while the account is flagged.

## How to deploy this set (notes for the team)

- **Match the angle to the warm segment.** Objection scripts (Angle 1) work best on **video viewers and engagers** who showed interest but did not click. The deep-dive (Angle 2) works best on **page and site visitors** who clicked but never added to cart, because their doubt is "what do I actually get". The case study (Angle 3) is the strongest all-rounder and the best first MOF test on any warm audience.
- **Six scripts is a starting battery, not a campaign.** Creative fatigue hits each ad around R700 to R1.5k of spend. At scale you burn 15 to 25 fresh creatives a week. These six are seeds: shoot multiple cuts of each (different hook lines, different founder, different B-roll) so one winning concept becomes ten variations. **Creative supply is the real ceiling, not budget.**
- **Compliance is the existential one.** Keep re-reading every cut against one test: does any frame, caption, or voiceover imply a guaranteed income? If yes, cut it. The account is already flagged. A ban ends the channel entirely, which is a far bigger loss than a slightly softer hook.
- **Judge on blended MER, not pixel ROAS.** In-app browsers strip the referrer to "direct" and the pixel is blind to WhatsApp closes by Sophia, so pixel ROAS will badly understate these ads. Read **Shopify revenue divided by ad spend** over a 3 to 7 day window. Breakeven sits around 1.16 to 1.2x.
- **Two non-creative blockers to flag before scaling spend behind these:** Yoco froze new online-payment activations, so confirm cash actually collects before pouring budget in, and confirm CAPI is properly set up so Meta has signal to optimise the warm audiences against.
- **Best windows:** Monday to Wednesday, evenings 19:00 to 22:00 SAST. Weight MOF delivery there.

## Traffic + Spend Math + Scale Ladder

## Traffic + Spend Math + Scale Ladder

This section turns the vibe goal ("I want 40 people on the site all day") into actual numbers: how many clicks that costs, what you have to spend to buy them, what profit those clicks have to throw off, and the week-by-week ramp to get there without torching the ad account or the margin.

Everything here is grounded in DropStore's real economics: ~95% gross margin, breakeven blended MER ~1.2x (Meta reports ROAS on ex-VAT spend but real cash out is x1.15 SA VAT, so 1.2x is breakeven not 1.05x), avg order ~R450, current ~R2.5k/day spend at ~1.7x blended MER, December peak ~2.1x at ~R9k/day (~R19k/day revenue).

---

### 1. The traffic goal, translated

The team is thinking in "concurrent live visitors." Meta and Shopify do not sell concurrency. They sell clicks. So first we convert.

**The conversion chain (concurrent visitors -> clicks/day):**

Concurrent visitors is just clicks/day x average session length, spread over the active hours of the day. The formula:

`concurrent ≈ (sessions per active hour) x (avg session minutes / 60)`

DropStore assumptions (all stated so you can argue with them):
- Avg session length: ~1.5 min. Mobile course-funnel traffic is short. 88% mobile, mostly in-app IG/FB browser, attention is thin.
- Active buying window where traffic actually clusters: ~14 hours/day (08:00 to 22:00 SAST). Meta does not serve evenly to 02:00, and you do not want it to.
- Sessions ≈ clicks (link clicks to the site), with ~10-15% bleed from clicks that never load. We will treat 1 paid click ≈ 0.9 sessions.

**Current state check (~5-10 concurrent now):**

At ~R2.5k/day spend and an SA in-app-browser CPC for this niche of ~R3.50 to R5.00, you are buying roughly 500 to 700 clicks/day = ~450-630 sessions/day. Spread over 14 hours at 1.5 min each, that is:

`(550 sessions / 14 hr) x (1.5/60) ≈ ~1 concurrent`... from paid alone. The 5-10 you actually see on the live tracker includes organic (which converts ~6x paid and is a big chunk of real traffic), direct, WhatsApp click-throughs, and the DROP10 popup crowd. That reconciles: paid is a minority of concurrent eyeballs, organic carries the rest.

**Target state (~40 concurrent all day):**

To hold ~40 concurrent across a 14-hour window at 1.5 min/session:

`40 = (sessions/hr) x (1.5/60)` -> sessions/hr = 1,600 -> **~22,400 sessions/day** -> **~25,000 link clicks/day** (gross, before the 10% bleed).

That is a 35-40x jump in clicks over today. Be honest with the room: 40 concurrent ALL DAY is a very large store. For context, ~22k sessions/day is high-traffic-Shopify territory.

**What that costs in pure paid terms (the scary number first):**

| CPC (SA, in-app, this niche) | Clicks needed/day for 40 concurrent | Pure ad spend/day |
|---|---|---|
| R3.50 (good creative, warm) | ~25,000 | ~R87,500 |
| R4.50 (blended realistic) | ~25,000 | ~R112,500 |
| R6.00 (cold/fatigued) | ~25,000 | ~R150,000 |

**This is the reality check the team needs.** Buying 40 concurrent purely with paid traffic costs R85k-150k/day. That is not the plan, and it should not be. The plan is R12-22k/day spend (below), which buys you maybe 8-15 concurrent from paid. The rest of "all-day 40" has to come from **organic + WhatsApp + return/popup traffic**, which is free and converts better. So the goal is not "buy 40 concurrent." It is "buy enough paid to hit the profit number, and let organic+Sophia stack on top toward the concurrency feel."

---

### 2. Honest note on "all day long" steady traffic

You cannot make concurrent traffic perfectly flat, and trying to is how you waste money.

- **Meta serves when converters are online.** The algo front-loads delivery into the hours your buyers actually buy. For DropStore that is Mon-Wed and the **19:00-22:00 SAST evening window**. If you force even 24-hour delivery (dayparting it flat), Meta has to show ads to worse audiences in dead hours (03:00-06:00) to spend the budget. Those clicks are cheaper but convert near zero. You will see "more concurrent at 4am" and "MER fell." That is the trade.
- **Realistic shape:** expect a curve. Mornings light, a midday bump, and a hard evening spike. Peak concurrent in the 20:00 hour might be 60-80 while the 10:00 hour sits at 15-20, averaging out toward the 40 "feel" without ever being flat.
- **What to actually optimize:** average concurrent across the buying window and depth of the evening peak, not a flat line. If you want the line less spiky, the lever is **organic content cadence** (post in mornings to pull free daytime traffic) and **WhatsApp/popup re-engagement**, not paid budget in dead hours.
- **Rule:** do not daypart to 24h to chase a flat tracker. Let Meta cluster spend into converting hours. A spiky-but-profitable day beats a flat-but-breakeven day every time.

### 3. The R10k/day PROFIT math (spend / revenue / MER table)

Profit here = revenue minus ad spend (incl 15% VAT on spend), at ~95% gross margin so we treat product COGS as negligible. Avg order ~R450.

Target: **R10,000 profit/day.**

| Blended MER | Revenue needed/day | Ad spend needed/day (ex-VAT) | Spend incl VAT (real cash) | Profit (rev − cash spend) | Orders/day (@R450) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **2.1x** (Dec peak) | ~R26,300 | ~R12,500 | ~R14,375 | ~R10,000 | ~58 |
| **1.7x** (current blended) | ~R34,500 | ~R20,300 | ~R23,300 | ~R10,000 | ~77 |
| **1.5x** (compressed at scale) | ~R45,000 | ~R30,000 | ~R34,500 | ~R10,000 | ~100 |

Read this top to bottom and the strategy writes itself: **the higher you can hold MER, the less you spend, the fewer orders you need, and the less cash-collection and creative-supply strain you take on.** R10k profit at 2.1x needs ~58 orders/day; at 1.5x it needs ~100. Every notch of MER you lose roughly doubles the operational load (more orders to fulfil, more Yoco transactions to clear, more fresh creative to feed). The whole game is **defending MER as you scale, not just adding spend.**

"Packages/day" assumes the R450 blended AOV holds. If the Growth tier (R449, most popular) stays the mix anchor and the R60 AI Studio upsell attach rate climbs, AOV drifts up and the order counts above drop. Watch AOV; it is a free MER lever.

### 4. The Scale Ladder (current ~R2.5k/day -> target), with the MER gate

You do not jump from R2.5k to R20k. You climb, and **every rung has a gate.** The gate is: blended MER (Shopify revenue / cash spend) must stay above the profit threshold for that rung before you add the next 20-30%. The Jun 3 collapse (MER fell 4.68 -> 1.62 when spend ~doubled into cold batches and a 0.47x CBO Scale) is exactly what the gate exists to prevent.

**Hard rules at every rung:**
- Step up only +20-30% at a time, and only when the set is **above breakeven AND pinning its budget cap** (if it underspends the cap, budget is not the constraint, creative is).
- **Cut any ad set under ~1.2x blended after 2-3 days.** Not 1 day (noise), not 5 days (bleeding). 2-3.
- Judge **blended** MER, never pixel ROAS. The pixel undercounts hard (in-app browsers strip referrer to "direct," and it is blind to WhatsApp/Sophia sales). Real MER = Shopify revenue / cash spend.
- Hold the homepage as the paid landing page (all 3 tiers, ~96% of orders land there). Never route paid to a single product page.

### 5. What MER realistically does as spend rises (compression) and the profit ceiling

MER does not hold flat as you scale. It compresses. This is physics, not pessimism:

- **At low spend** you are skimming the warmest, highest-intent pocket (people already half-sold on AI-builds-your-store). High MER, ~2-4x on the best sets, was the May 23-Jun 2 reality (MER 4.68 on ~R13k cumulative).
- **As spend rises** Meta must reach colder audiences to spend the money. Each incremental rand buys a worse click. The Jun 3-9 window proved it: incremental spend into new batches + a 0.47x CBO dragged blended MER to 1.62 while revenue stayed flat (~R5.6k/day). **The extra spend did not break anything; it bought colder traffic.**
- **Realistic compression curve for DropStore:**

| Daily spend (ex-VAT) | Realistic blended MER | Profit/day |
|---|---|---|
| R2.5k | ~1.7-2.1x | ~R1.4-2.8k |
| R9k (Dec proven) | ~2.1x peak, ~1.7x typical | ~R8.6k at peak, ~R4-6k typical |
| R12k | ~1.7x if creative fresh, ~1.5x if not | ~R6.6k / ~R4k |
| R20k+ | ~1.5x likely, ~1.6-1.7x only with elite creative + audience diversification | ~R6.7k / drops fast below 1.5x |

**The profit ceiling.** With current creative supply and account constraints, the realistic sustained profit ceiling is roughly **R6-9k/day at R12-15k spend**, and R10k/day profit is reachable but sits right at the edge: it needs MER held at ~1.6-1.7x on R18-22k spend, which only happens if you out-produce creative fatigue. The math says R10k profit is real. The operations say it is fragile. Plan for ~R7k/day sustainable and treat R10k as the stretch day, not the floor.

**Four things that cap the ceiling harder than spend does (the team is under-weighting these):**

1. **The ad account is already FLAGGED (read-only for automated changes).** Aggressive "make R10k / make money" income-claim creative is the single fastest way to get a money-opportunity account restricted or banned by Meta. If the account dies, the ceiling is R0. **Hard rule: no income-claim hooks in the scaling creative.** Lead with the AI-builds-your-store outcome ("First AI System," "Should be asking abt AI" are the proven winners and are compliant), never with a rand figure. This constraint outranks every number above.
2. **Creative supply is the true bottleneck.** Fatigue hits each creative around R700-1.5k cumulative spend. At R12-22k/day you burn through **15-25 fresh ads/week** (see the ladder column). If founder-led + student-UGC production cannot sustain that, MER falls regardless of budget. Creative pipeline is the real scale lever.
3. **Yoco froze new online-payment activations.** Rising revenue means rising transaction volume through a payment processor under strain. Confirm clearing capacity BEFORE week 3-4 of the ladder, or you scale revenue you cannot collect.
4. **Pixel undercount / CAPI gap.** If you scale on pixel ROAS you will cut winning sets that Sophia is actually closing on WhatsApp. Get CAPI properly set up and judge blended only. Undercounting will make a 1.7x set look like 1.2x and trick you into killing your best performer.

## Landing Page, CRO, AOV and Offer: The Traffic Multiplier

## The Traffic Multiplier: Fix the Page Before You Feed the Beast

Everything in this section happens BEFORE you push spend from R2.5k/day toward R12-22k/day. Here is the uncomfortable truth: right now 97% of people who land on DropStore never even add to cart. Sessions-to-add-to-cart sits at roughly 3%, and it is worst on mobile, which is where 88% of your traffic lives (Instagram and Facebook in-app browsers). You are about to pour 5-9x more paid traffic into a page that leaks 97 of every 100 visitors. That is not scaling. That is setting money on fire faster.

### Why CRO comes first, and the exact leverage

Think of the funnel as a multiplication chain: traffic x add-to-cart rate x checkout rate x payment success = sales. Paid traffic only touches the first number. The site touches every number after it. When you double spend, you double one factor. When you double add-to-cart rate, you multiply the entire downstream chain, including all the future spend you have not made yet.

The math on effective CAC (cost to acquire a customer):

| Lever | Add-to-cart rate | Cost per add-to-cart at R2.5k/day | Effective CAC direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Today | 3% | baseline | baseline |
| Fix the leak | 6% | halved | roughly half the CAC |
| Fix the leak, scaled to R12k/day | 6% | same low cost, 5x volume | you can profit at a far lower ROAS |

Doubling add-to-cart roughly halves effective CAC because you get twice the buyers from the same spend. With ~95% gross margin and breakeven ROAS of only ~1.2x, every point of conversion you recover drops almost straight to profit. A CRO win is a permanent discount on all future ad spend. A creative win fatigues out at R700-1.5k. The page fix does not fatigue.

Spend the first week fixing the page. Then open the taps.

### Homepage CRO fixes (the page that converts 96% of orders)

The homepage shows all three tiers (Starter R299, Growth R449, Elite R999) and converts ~96% of orders. Do NOT route paid traffic to a single product page. Route everything to the homepage. These are the fixes, in priority order.

**1. Hero clarity in 3 seconds on a phone.** Someone tapping through from an Instagram story decides in about 3 seconds. Above the fold on a 6-inch screen they must instantly get: what it is, what they get, and one button. The winning angle is already proven in your creatives ("First AI System", "Should be asking abt AI"), so the page must match it.
- Headline: "We build your whole online store with AI. You learn to run it."
- Subline: "Pick a package. Our AI builds the store. You start selling. No tech skills needed."
- One primary button: "See the packages" or "Start now". Not three competing buttons.
- The three tiers should sit just below the fold, not crammed into it. Lead with the promise, then reveal the offer.

**2. The DROP10 popup timing (this is hurting you on mobile).** The DROP10 popup became roughly half of orders, so it stays. But on mobile it currently covers the hero, which means the in-app-browser visitor sees a coupon box over a page they have not understood yet, and many just bounce. Fix the trigger, not the popup:
- Delay it to fire at around 12-15 seconds OR on scroll past 40% of the page, whichever comes first.
- Add exit-intent on mobile (fire when the user scrolls up fast or moves toward the back gesture), so it catches the leaver instead of blocking the arriver.
- Never let it cover the primary CTA or the tier cards. It should slide up from the bottom as a sticky bar on mobile, not a full-screen takeover.

**3. CTA contrast.** The buy buttons must be the single highest-contrast element on the page. One bold accent colour, used nowhere else, on every CTA. The Growth R449 "most popular" card gets a visual flag (badge, border, slight scale) so the eye lands on the one you most want sold. Buttons must be thumb-sized and reachable one-handed on mobile.

**4. Trust and proof above the fold.** A money-related offer from an unknown brand triggers instant skepticism. Put proof where they see it before they scroll:
- A line of real student-win UGC or a count ("Join hundreds of South Africans already building").
- Founder faces (Henro and Seth) early, because founder-led is what is working in your content. Trust transfers from the creative to the page only if the faces match.
- A short trust strip: secure checkout, instant access, built for South Africa.

**5. Message match from ad to page.** This is the silent killer with in-app browsers. If the ad says "AI builds your whole store" and the page leads with "online dropshipping course", the visitor feels a mismatch in the first second and leaves. The homepage hero must use the SAME words and the SAME visual as the top creatives. When you test a new ad angle, the page hero should echo it. One ad concept, one matching page promise.

A note on measurement: the pixel undercounts badly. In-app browsers strip the referrer to "direct" and the pixel is blind to WhatsApp sales that Sophia closes. Judge every CRO change on BLENDED MER (Shopify revenue divided by ad spend), not pixel ROAS. If CAPI (the Conversions API) is not fully set up, getting it wired is part of this work, because better signal also feeds Meta better and lowers CAC on its own.

### AOV levers (raise the average order so you can afford a lower ROAS)

Average order is ~R450. Every rand you add to AOV lets you survive a lower ROAS and still profit, because the margin is ~95%. These are the levers, easiest first.

**1. Order bump at checkout.** A single tick-box on the cart or checkout: "Add priority AI Studio build for R60 so your store is ready first." This is the R60 skip, repositioned as a bump. Bumps convert far higher than upsell pages because there is zero extra friction.

**2. The R60 AI Studio skip, framed as scarcity not admin.** Right now it reads like a queue fee. Reframe it as a benefit: "Skip the line. Your store gets built first, today, for R60." Same price, stronger pull.

**3. Tier-upgrade prompts on the tier cards.** On the Starter and Growth cards, a small nudge: "For just R150 more, Growth gives you [the thing they actually want]." Anchor against Elite so Growth looks like the smart middle. Growth is already your most-popular, so make the step up to it obvious and the step from Growth to Elite tempting.

**4. Post-purchase upsell.** Immediately after payment, on the thank-you page, offer one thing they could not get before buying: a done-with-you setup call, an extra niche, a bonus product pack, or the AI Studio skip if they did not take it. Post-purchase upsells are pure AOV because the buyer has already trusted you with their card.

Stacked, these can lift AOV from ~R450 toward R550-650 without a single extra visitor, which directly lowers the ROAS you need to hit R10k profit/day.

### Offer design (offer beats creative beats targeting for scale)

You can out-create and out-target a weak offer for a while, but a strong offer is what lets you scale spend without the ROAS falling apart. Build the offer so the decision feels obvious.

**1. Guarantee (de-risk the buy).** South Africans are cautious with online "make money" type buys, partly because the space is full of scams. A clean guarantee removes the biggest objection: "If your AI store is not built and live within 48 hours, we sort it or refund you." Make it about delivery and effort, not income. Never guarantee earnings.

**2. Bonuses (stack value, do not drop price).** Add bonuses to each tier instead of discounting: a ready-to-sell product pack, a 7-day launch checklist, a WhatsApp support group, a winning-ads swipe file. Bonuses raise perceived value while protecting your ~95% margin.

**3. Urgency (real, not fake).** Real urgency only. "AI build slots are limited this week" is true if you cap builds. A live countdown on the DROP10 code is fine if it actually expires. Never use fake stock counters or permanent "ending tonight" timers. False urgency erodes the trust you just built above the fold.

**4. Returning-visitor escalation (the smart, compliant version).** Most people do not buy on visit one. Escalate the offer instead of repeating it:
- Visit 1: DROP10 popup (the proven half-of-orders driver). Code DROP10, framed as a welcome.
- Return visit (cookie or matched WhatsApp number): a one-time DROP15, framed as final. "You came back, so here is our best offer. DROP15 is once-off and will not come again."
- The escalation must be genuinely once-off. If DROP15 keeps reappearing, it is just a permanent price cut and customers learn to wait for it.

Suggested copy for the returning-visitor offer, no em dashes:

> You are back. Good. Here is the best we do: DROP15 for 15% off any package. This one is once-off, so if you have been on the fence, now is the time. Pick your package below.

**Compliance guardrail, and this is existential.** The Meta ad account is ALREADY flagged and currently read-only for automated changes. Aggressive income-claim creative ("make R10k", "guaranteed profit") is exactly what gets money-opportunity accounts restricted or banned. So:
- Offer copy and ad copy sell the BUILD and the SKILL, never the income. "We build your store with AI" is safe. "Make R10k a month" is an account-killer.
- The R10k/day profit target is YOUR internal goal. It must never appear in customer-facing copy, popups, or ads.
- Keep guarantees about delivery and effort, never earnings.

### Abandoned-cart recovery (R70k+ is sitting in carts, ~1 recovered)

There is over R70k stuck in abandoned carts and roughly one recovery to show for it. That is the single biggest pile of nearly-free money in the business. The recovery system is already built and was activated on 2026-06-10, so this is about pointing the existing machine at the cart backlog, not building from scratch.

What is already live (DropStore platform):
- The sender (recovery-send.ts) goes through the 360dialog WhatsApp API.
- The engine (recovery-tick cron) targets recovery sessions, respects phone consent, idle timing, and a suppression list, and is idempotent per (session, step) so nobody gets spammed twice.
- The first real blast (193 popup signups, the discount15 / WA15 template from Sophia) sent 193 with 0 failures and got ~18% replying within 12 minutes, with Sophia auto-handling the conversations.

What to do now:

**1. Two-channel recovery: email plus WhatsApp.** Email is the wide net, WhatsApp is the closer. Note the live caveat: the DropStore transactional email (sa.dropstore@gmail.com) had an OAuth outage from 2026-06-11. Email recovery is dead until that account is recovered (new password, then re-auth the gog token). Get that fixed, because email recovery is half the channel.

**2. WhatsApp/Sophia sequence on the real cart backlog.** The first blast hit popup signups with no cart. Now point the engine at actual abandoned CARTS (people who added items and left), where intent is far higher. Suggested cadence (the engine already supports step timing):
- Step 1, ~1 hour after abandon: a soft nudge. "Hey, saw you were checking out the [tier] package. Want me to answer any questions before you grab it?"
- Step 2, ~12 hours: the offer. "Still keen? Here is WA15 for 15% off your package. Once-off."
- Step 3, ~24-48 hours: last call, then stop. "Last nudge from me. WA15 closes today. Want me to set your store up?"
- Let Sophia handle every reply, because she closes sales the pixel never sees.

**3. Per-session, not per-phone, and respect consent.** The engine sends per session, so a number with two sessions can get the message twice. De-dupe on phone before a blast. Only message numbers with phone_consent=true, honour the suppression list, and keep an easy opt-out. This is both POPIA-sensible and protects the number, which is SHARED with the live Sophia gateway.

**4. Recover the R70k, then keep it running.** A one-time sweep of the existing R70k backlog at even a modest recovery rate is several thousand rand of near-pure margin. After the sweep, leave the sequence on so every future abandon gets worked automatically. With ~95% margin, recovered carts are almost entirely profit, which is the cheapest ROAS lift available before you spend a cent more on ads.

### The order of operations

1. Fix the homepage (hero clarity, popup timing, CTA contrast, proof, message match) and wire CAPI.
2. Add the AOV levers (order bump, R60 skip reframe, tier upsell, post-purchase).
3. Lock the offer (guarantee, bonuses, real urgency, compliant returning-visitor escalation).
4. Fix the email outage and point the live recovery engine at the real cart backlog.
5. ONLY THEN scale spend.

Do this and the same ad rand buys roughly twice the buyers, every recovered cart is free margin, and you reach R10k profit/day on far less spend and far less risk to the flagged ad account.

## Compliance, Account-Risk and Tracking

This is the section that can end DropStore overnight. Everything else in this plan assumes the ad account stays alive and the numbers we optimise on are real. Both of those are currently at risk. Read this before spending a cent more.

Two hard truths up front:

Money-opportunity products that shout income claims are the single most common reason these accounts go from "flagged" to "permanently disabled." ### 1. Meta ad policy on income claims (the thing that gets us banned)

DropStore sits in the exact vertical Meta flags hardest: **"make money online / work from home / business opportunity."** Meta's own 2025 guidance explicitly names "finance, crypto, investment education, and work-from-home offers" as facing heightened scrutiny "even when the advertiser is legitimate." A dropshipping course that promises income is textbook.

The rule Meta enforces, in plain terms: **you may sell the skill and the process. You may not promise the outcome, the amount, or the timeframe.**

The trigger words and patterns that get money-opportunity accounts restricted or banned:

- Specific Rand amounts tied to a result: "Make R10k/month," "R50k in 90 days," "R450 a sale on autopilot."
- Guarantees or certainty: "guaranteed income," "you WILL make money," "passive income guaranteed."
- Timeframe + money: "quit your job in 30 days," "first sale this week."
- Implied effortlessness on income: "make money while you sleep," "earn without lifting a finger."
- "Before/after my bank balance" screenshots, Shopify revenue dashboards shown as a promise of what the buyer gets, Yoco/payout notification screenshots framed as the buyer's future.
- Personal income testimonials presented as typical results ("Henro made R340k") without making clear it is one result, not what a buyer should expect.

**This matters double for us because our proven winners flirt with the line.** "First AI System" and "Should be asking abt AI" win because the angle is the AI building the store, not an income promise. Keep them there. The moment a fatigued creative gets "refreshed" by a writer adding "and start making R10k/month," we are handing Meta the reason to kill an already-flagged account.

#### DO / DON'T phrasing table (DropStore-specific)

| Theme | DON'T (gets the account restricted) | DO (safe, on-brand, still converts) |
|---|---|---|
| The core offer | "Make R10k/month dropshipping" | "We build your whole online store with AI. You learn how to run it." |
| The AI angle (our winner) | "AI that prints money for you" | "An AI system builds your store for you. From scratch. In minutes." |
| Speed | "First sale in 7 days, guaranteed" | "Skip the months of figuring it out. Start with a store that's already built." |
| Earnings proof | Screenshot of a R340k Shopify dashboard captioned "this could be you" | "This is what Henro built. We'll teach you the same system." (real result, clearly one person's, framed as learning) |
| Effort | "Passive income while you sleep" | "Run it from your phone in your spare time." (effort honest, no income claim) |
| Outcome | "Replace your salary" / "quit your 9 to 5" | "Build something of your own on the side." |
| Urgency | "Last chance to get rich before prices rise" | "Founding-member pricing closes soon." (urgency on price, not on income) |
| Tier framing | "R999 turns into R50k" | "Elite gets you the done-for-you build plus the full course." (value, not multiplier) |

The shift in every row is the same: **move from PROMISE to PROCESS, from AMOUNT to ABILITY.** Meta's reviewers (human and automated) are trained to flag the promise. They do not penalise "here is a skill and a tool."

Landing-page rule: **the site must not contradict the ad.** Meta reviews the destination, not just the creative. If the ad is clean but the homepage, the DROP10 popup, or a testimonial block screams "MAKE R10k," the whole funnel gets flagged and the clean ad goes down with it. Audit the homepage, the popup copy, and every testimonial for income claims before scaling spend. This is non-negotiable on an already-flagged account.

One more: **Special Ad Category does not apply to us here.** That regime (housing/employment/credit) is mandatory for US/Canada/EU-targeted ads. We target South Africa, so we are not forced to self-classify. That is not a free pass. The income-claim content rules above apply globally and are what actually threaten the account.

### 2. The account is already flagged: de-risk BEFORE going aggressive

Read-only-for-automation status means Meta has restricted our ability to make programmatic changes. Practically: the account is on a watchlist, one or two more policy strikes likely tips it to permanent disable, and at that point we lose the pixel's learning history, the audiences, and the ad account in one shot. **Step 4: Only then go aggressive, and split risk.**

When scaling toward R12-22k/day, do not run all spend through one account. Run the proven clean winners across both the (hopefully un-flagged) live account and the warmed backup, so a single disable never takes 100% of delivery offline. Keep both squeaky clean. The flag does not get cleared by good behaviour for a week and then forgotten; it is a permanent "strike one." Behave like it.

**Hard gate: do not increase spend on the flagged account until (a) all income-claim creative and landing-page copy is removed, and (b) the backup BM exists and is warming.** Aggression before insurance is the single highest-risk move on the table.

### 3. Pixel + Conversions API (CAPI): mandatory before scaling

Right now we judge on blended MER because the pixel undercounts. That is the correct workaround, but it is a workaround. The reason to fix tracking properly is not reporting vanity. **Better signal makes Meta's optimisation better, which lowers cost per sale, which is real money at R12-22k/day.**

#### Why the pixel undercounts for DropStore specifically

- **88% of our traffic is mobile, mostly via Instagram/Facebook in-app browsers.** In-app browsers strip the referrer and break cookie persistence, so a chunk of conversions get logged as "direct" and never attributed to the ad that drove them. This is the biggest leak for us specifically.
- **iOS / ATT** suppresses browser-pixel firing and shortens attribution windows.
- **WhatsApp / Sophia-closed sales are completely invisible to the pixel.** When a fence-sitter clicks the ad, lands, leaves, then closes in WhatsApp with Sophia, the browser pixel never sees a Purchase. Meta thinks that ad failed. It did not. (This is also why organic appears to convert ~6x paid: a lot of paid-driven WhatsApp closes get miscredited.)

Net effect: Meta is optimising on a fraction of our true Purchases. It is steering budget using bad data. CAPI fixes this by sending the Purchase **server-side**, where in-app browsers and iOS cannot strip it.

#### What to do, in order

**a) Turn on Shopify's Conversions API (hybrid pixel + CAPI).**

Shopify's native Facebook & Instagram channel sends server-side events directly to Meta and **auto-deduplicates** against the browser pixel using a shared `event_id`. Best practice is the hybrid setup (browser pixel + server CAPI both firing), so if one path is blocked the other still lands, and Meta merges them into one event.

- In Shopify, open the Facebook & Instagram sales channel, confirm Data Sharing is set to **Maximum** (this is what enables the server-side CAPI path and Advanced Matching).
- Confirm the correct (live) pixel/dataset is connected.

**b) Verify CAPI is actually on (do not assume).**

In Meta Events Manager, with the pixel selected:

- **Test Events tab:** open the live store in another tab, view product, add to cart, start checkout, do one test purchase. Confirm AddToCart and Purchase appear in real time.
- For each event, confirm it shows **"Processed" from both Browser and Server**, and that deduplication is working (one merged event, not two). If you see double-counted Purchases, the `event_id` is not matching and dedup is broken. Shopify's native integration normally handles this automatically; if it is broken, the channel is misconfigured.
- Check the **Diagnostics tab** for warnings (missing event_id, low match quality, deprecated parameters).

**c) Event deduplication.**

Dedup works when the browser pixel event and the server CAPI event carry the **same `event_id`**. Same `event_id` = Meta counts one Purchase. Mismatched or missing = double counting (inflated, useless ROAS) or no merge. With Shopify native, this is automatic; with any third-party CAPI app (e.g. an Attribuly-type tool), the `event_id` mapping must be verified manually or we get garbage numbers.

**d) Event Match Quality (EMQ): send hashed email + phone.**

EMQ is Meta's score (0-10) for how well it can match a conversion to a real person. Higher EMQ = more conversions correctly attributed = better optimisation. For DropStore this is a big lever because every Shopify checkout already collects email and phone.

- Target **EMQ 7+** after CAPI is live.
- Ensure email, phone, first/last name, and address are passed (Shopify hashes them before sending; we never send raw PII).
- Our phone numbers are gold here: SA mobile numbers are a strong match key, and they are the same identifier Sophia uses on WhatsApp. Make sure phone is in the payload.

**e) Domain verification + Aggregated Event Measurement.**

- Verify the store domain in Business Settings (DNS TXT, meta-tag, or file upload). Unverified = we cannot control event priority and iOS attribution degrades further.
- Configure the 8 Aggregated Event Measurement events with **Purchase as priority 1.** This is what preserves Purchase attribution for iOS users.

#### The advanced move: fire a server-side CAPI Purchase when a WhatsApp/Sophia sale closes

This is the highest-leverage tracking upgrade we can make, and it directly attacks our biggest blind spot.

Today, when Sophia closes a sale in WhatsApp, Meta sees nothing, so the ad that originally drove that lead gets zero credit and Meta learns "this ad does not convert." It then under-delivers our actual best ads.

The fix: when a WhatsApp/Sophia sale closes, **send a server-side CAPI Purchase event from our backend** (the Sophia gateway already handles these flows), with:

- The customer's **hashed phone and email** (Sophia has the phone; that is the match key).
- The order value and currency (ZAR).
- A consistent `event_id` so it dedupes against the Shopify order if that order also fires a pixel/CAPI event (avoid double-counting the same sale).
- Ideally the original click identifiers (`fbc` / `fbp`) if we captured them when the lead first hit the site, which closes the loop from ad click to WhatsApp close.

Do this carefully. We must not double-count a sale that Shopify already reported. The rule: one Purchase per order, deduped by a stable `event_id`. Sales that ONLY happen in WhatsApp (never touched Shopify checkout) are the ones we are recovering; sales that go through Shopify checkout are already covered, so the WhatsApp fire must not duplicate them.

Caution on the flagged account: feeding server-side Purchases is normal and encouraged by Meta, but only send **real, verifiable** conversions. Never inflate the feed to juice ROAS. On a watched account, suspicious server-event patterns are another flag risk.

---

### 4. How tracking improvements directly raise reported ROAS and optimisation quality

This is the business case for treating tracking as a revenue project, not IT housekeeping.

1. **Recovered attribution = higher reported ROAS on the same spend.** Every WhatsApp/Sophia close and every in-app-browser sale we make visible to Meta is revenue that already happened but was uncredited. Surfacing it raises measured ROAS without spending an extra Rand. Our blended MER (1.7x) is the truth; the goal is to drag Meta's *measured* number up toward that truth so we stop under-trusting our own ads.

2. **Better signal = better optimisation = lower cost per sale.** Meta's algorithm optimises toward the Purchases it can see. Feed it more complete, higher-EMQ Purchase data and it finds buyers more accurately, which cuts CPA. At R2.5k/day a few percent CPA improvement is minor; at R12-22k/day it compounds into real daily profit toward the R10k/day goal.

3. **Higher EMQ widens effective reach.** Strong match quality lets Meta confidently attribute and therefore confidently scale delivery. Weak match quality makes Meta cautious, which throttles spend efficiency exactly when we want to push hard.

4. **It fixes the "organic converts 6x paid" illusion.** A lot of that gap is paid-driven traffic that closed in WhatsApp and got miscredited to organic/direct. Proper CAPI + the WhatsApp Purchase fire re-credits those sales to the ads that earned them, so we stop under-investing in paid and over-crediting organic.

**Sequencing:** CAPI verification and the WhatsApp Purchase fire should happen **before** the big spend ramp, not during it. Scaling on broken signal means Meta scales the wrong ads and we burn budget learning what we already know is wrong. Fix the signal, then pour fuel.

One external constraint to flag alongside this: **Yoco recently froze new online-payment activations.** That is a cash-collection bottleneck, not a Meta issue, but it sits in the same risk bucket. There is no point optimising Meta to drive R25-37k/day in revenue if the payment processor cannot collect it. Confirm Yoco capacity (or a backup processor) is in place before the revenue ramp, or we generate demand we cannot bank.

## Ops and Cash-Flow Capacity at Scale: What Breaks When DropStore Goes 5x

## Ops and Cash-Flow Capacity at Scale: What Breaks When Volume 5x's

Today DropStore runs ~R2.5k/day spend at MER ~1.7x, so roughly R4.2k/day revenue and ~9 to 10 orders/day at the R450 average. The R10k profit/day target means R12k to R22k/day spend, R25k to R37k/day revenue, and **55 to 120 new paying students every single day**. At 95% margin the money side is beautiful. The problem is that almost nothing in the ops, payment, and support stack was built for that throughput. The first thing that breaks at 5x is not the ads. It is the ability to collect the cash and deliver the build without the new buyer refunding.

Below is what cracks, in order of how badly it bites.

---

### 1. PAYMENT / CASH FLOW (the hard blocker, fix this first)

You cannot scale revenue you cannot collect. Yoco has frozen new online-payment activations, and DropStore checkout (card / Apple Pay / PayFast routed through Yoco on www.dropstoresa.co.za) depends on it. If Yoco throttles, holds, or reviews the account once daily volume jumps from ~R4k to R30k+, the funnel keeps converting but the money stops landing. You would be paying Meta R20k/day to drive traffic to a checkout that cannot bank the proceeds. That is the fastest way to blow up.

**Why it is existential, not annoying:**
- A sudden 7x jump in transaction count and value on a payment account is exactly the pattern that triggers a processor risk review. "Money-making / online income" merchants get flagged harder than average. A 24 to 72 hour hold while they "verify the business" at R30k/day is R30k to R90k of stranded cash plus a checkout that may be showing errors to live buyers.
- The Meta account is already flagged and read-only for automated changes. If checkout also chokes, you have both the demand engine and the cash register wobbling at the same time. Do not scale into that.

**Cash-flow timing gap (the part the team is probably not modelling):** You pay Meta **daily** (and ad spend carries +15% VAT in real cash out), but Yoco/PayFast pay out on a **delay**, typically T+1 to T+2 business days, longer over weekends and SA public holidays. At R20k/day spend that means you are routinely floating **R40k to R60k+** of ad money before the matching revenue settles into the bank. Monday to Wednesday are your strongest sales days and the buying window is 19:00 to 22:00 SAST, so a big Wednesday night of sales does not pay out until Friday, while Thursday and Friday ad spend has already gone out the door. A few strong days back to back can drain the operating account even though the business is wildly profitable on paper.

**Mitigation, do before pushing spend past ~R6k/day:**
1. **Resolve Yoco status in writing.** Confirm the account is fully activated for online payments at the new volume, and ask them directly: what daily/monthly turnover triggers a review or a rolling reserve, and what is the payout schedule. Get the number before you hit it, not after.
2. **Stand up a backup gateway now, live and tested, not "we'll sort it if it breaks."** PayFast is the obvious SA-native fallback (already in the Yoco stack as an option) and Stripe is the second. Have a second processor wired into Shopify checkout and verified with a real R10 test order, so if Yoco holds you flip the primary gateway in minutes, not days. A frozen single processor with no backup is a single point of failure on your only cash-collection path.
3. **Fund the float.** Hold a cash buffer of at least 3 to 4 days of peak ad spend (call it R80k to R100k at R20k/day) in the operating account, or arrange a short-term facility, so payout delay never forces you to throttle winning ads mid-scale. The worst outcome is pausing a 3.4x ad set because the bank is dry while R60k sits in Yoco settlement.
4. **Watch payout timing vs spend daily.** Add a simple "cash position vs settlement pipeline" line to dropfinances: today's spend committed, revenue collected, revenue in settlement, bank balance. Scaling blind to this is how a profitable store runs out of money.

### 2. Fulfilment / onboarding load (where new buyers churn or refund)

55 to 120 new students/day each trigger the same chain: payment lands, dashboard access email goes out, they claim/log in, they run AI Studio to build a store, and some need support. Every weak link in that chain at 5x volume becomes a wall of refund requests, because a R299 to R999 buyer who paid tonight and cannot get in or cannot build their store wants their money back **tonight**.

**What is already shaky and must be hardened before scaling:**

- **AI Studio outages.** AI Studio / Niche Finder / Store Builder are routed through the AOS Mac Mini (`/ai/*`), because the Vercel AI Gateway is blocked pending a card on file. That means the single resident Mac Mini is the entire fulfilment engine for the "AI builds your whole store" promise, which is the proven top creative. At 55 to 120 builds/day, plus the R60 "skip the line / AI Studio" upsell explicitly selling speed, that box is the bottleneck. If it falls over during the 19:00 to 22:00 buying rush, dozens of buyers who just paid hit a dead Studio. **Harden:** unblock the Vercel AI Gateway (or a hosted fallback) so AI Studio has a cloud failover instead of one Mac Mini; add a queue with a clear "your build is processing, here is your slot" state so a busy Studio degrades gracefully instead of erroring; alert the moment Studio error rate or latency climbs. The Store Builder async work (heavy build moved off-Vercel to a resident worker behind `STORE_BUILDER_ASYNC`) is exactly the right direction, get it live before scaling because the synchronous path was already throwing 502/524s.
- **Claim / login friction.** There has been recurring auth friction: the logout cookie bug, signout not clearing the cookie, the buy-vs-dashboard URL confusion (Sophia once sent a lead to amalfiai.com to check out instead of dropstoresa.co.za). At 9 orders/day a stuck login is a WhatsApp message Sophia smooths over. At 100 orders/day it is a refund queue. **Harden:** make the post-payment flow bulletproof, paid → instant access email with the correct dashboard link → one-tap claim that works on mobile in the Instagram/Facebook in-app browser (88% of traffic is mobile, much of it in-app browsers that mangle cookies and referrers). Test the full paid-to-logged-in journey specifically inside the IG/FB in-app browser, not just Safari. Send the access link by WhatsApp as well as email, since these buyers live on WhatsApp and SA email deliverability to gmail junk is a known issue.
- **Account claims at volume.** Verify the access email actually sends and lands for every paid order without manual touch. A digital product where 1 in 10 buyers does not receive access is a 10% refund rate baked in.

**The onboarding rule for scaling:** a buyer must be able to go from "paid" to "inside the dashboard with a store building" with zero human intervention, on a mobile in-app browser, in under two minutes, even when 30 other people are doing the same thing. Until that is true end to end, every R1k of extra ad spend buys you proportionally more refunds.

**Inbound volume.** 5x the traffic means 5x the WhatsApp inbound, and it concentrates in the same 19:00 to 22:00 window on Monday to Wednesday. The model side scales fine, but watch the constraints around it:
- **360dialog template / messaging tier and rate limits.** A spike in outbound (DROP10 closers, cart recovery) into the same nightly window can hit messaging limits or get the number quality-rated down. A degraded WhatsApp number kills your best non-pixel closer right when volume is highest.

- 360dialog number quality rating and messaging tier (watch for downgrades).

### 4. Refund / chargeback risk at scale

Aggressive acquisition plus a digital, money-making product is a chargeback magnet, and the risk compounds with volume in a way that can take out the payment account independently of everything above.

- **Buyer's remorse on a money-opportunity product.** A meaningful slice of cold IG/FB buyers in the 19:00 to 22:00 impulse window will wake up and want a refund, especially if AI Studio was down or login failed that night (see section 2). At 9 orders/day that is a handful. At 100/day, even a 5% refund/dispute rate is 5+ disputes daily.
- **Chargebacks endanger the processor relationship.** Card networks flag merchants above roughly a 1% chargeback ratio. A flagged or held processor is the same existential cash-collection failure as the Yoco freeze. Aggressive scaling plus weak onboarding is exactly how you cross that line.
- **It feeds the Meta flag too.** High refund/dispute signals and income-claim creative are both inputs Meta uses to restrict money-opportunity accounts, and the account is already read-only. Refund spikes make the ad-account problem worse.

**Mitigation:**
- Fix onboarding first (section 2). The cheapest refund prevention is a buyer who actually got in and built a store the same night.
- Clear, honest checkout copy and a stated refund policy so disputes route to you, not the bank. A buyer who emails you for a refund is recoverable; a chargeback is a fee plus a ratio hit.
- Instant value delivery: the faster they get the course + a built store after paying, the lower the remorse window. The R60 "skip the line" upsell makes the speed promise explicit, so under-delivering on it directly drives refunds.
- Track refund rate and chargeback ratio as first-class daily metrics in dropfinances, with an alert threshold. If refunds climb past ~5% or disputes past ~0.7%, slow acquisition before the processor does it for you.

At 5x the daily ad-account, payment, and support work cannot all sit on Josh plus two founders doing it between other things. The honest constraint: **creative supply is the true ceiling** (each creative fatigues at ~R700 to R1.5k spend, so R12k to R22k/day burns 15 to 25 fresh ads/week), and the ops below must run reliably enough that nobody is firefighting instead of filming.

To feed 15 to 25 fresh ads/week you need a repeatable pipeline: founders filming founder-led angles, a steady inflow of student-win UGC (incentivise students to send wins), and someone editing and shipping daily. One person filming ad hoc cannot sustain this. **Important guardrail:** the winning angle is "AI builds your whole store," not "make R10k." Aggressive income-claim creative is what gets money-opportunity ad accounts restricted, and this one is already flagged. Keep the volume of fresh creative high but keep the claims on the AI/done-for-you angle, not the money.
- **Daily account watcher (one named person, every day).** Someone owns the morning and evening check: blended MER from Shopify revenue / spend (never trust pixel ROAS, in-app browsers strip referrer to direct and WhatsApp sales are invisible), budget allocation per the locked play (feed only sets above breakeven that are pinning their cap), cash position vs settlement, payment-gateway health, and the Meta account's flag status. This is a real daily job at R20k/day, not a glance.
- **Support / spike cover (nightly, 19:00 to 22:00).** The buying window is also the support window. Someone must cover WhatsApp manual-takeover (the dropfinances Messages send bar), login/claim issues, and AI Studio failures live during peak Monday to Wednesday. At 100 buyers/night, a 10% hiccup rate is 10 people needing help in three hours, every strong night.
- **CAPI / measurement owner.** The pixel undercounts badly and CAPI is likely not fully set up. Before scaling spend, someone needs to get the Conversions API live so Meta's optimisation and your reporting are not flying blind, otherwise you are scaling a budget against numbers that understate true performance and you will misallocate.

**Bottom line:** the math to R10k profit/day works at 95% margin. What breaks at 5x is everything between the click and the cash: a single frozen payment processor with no backup, a single Mac Mini running every AI build, login friction on mobile in-app browsers, a WhatsApp number that can get rate-limited or quality-downgraded in the nightly rush, a refund/chargeback rate that can take out the processor, and a creative pipeline that cannot feed 20 fresh ads a week. Fix payments and onboarding before you touch the budget. Then scale spend in steps, watching cash position and the Meta flag, not pixel ROAS.

The Architecture doc's own footnote ("more ad sets than this starves each one below the ~50 weekly threshold") quietly concedes the point against itself.

**Action:** Pick ONE structure and write it as the canonical launch, do not ship both. Given the flag, the leaky funnel, the CBO-at-0.47x burn history, AND the Andromeda reality, the defensible synthesis is: start CONSOLIDATED (1 broad Advantage+ prospecting campaign + 1 retargeting ad set, per Andromeda) for budget liquidity and learning, NOT the 6-adset tree. Keep ABO discipline only as a controlled test lane once you have liquidity, not as the default. Reconcile the two docs or the team runs whichever they read last.

### 6. Testing methodology has no statistical rigor. "Kill at 48-72h / R700-1,000 spent" is a recipe for killing winners as noise.
At avg order R450, R700-1,000 of spend on a new ad is ONE TO TWO purchases. Judging an ad on 1-2 conversion events is pure noise, you will kill genuine winners that happened to get a quiet first day and keep duds that got a fluke sale. The Spend Math doc gets closer ("not 1 day, noise"), but the Campaign Architecture kill table still pulls the trigger at a sample size with zero statistical power. Worse, the in-app-browser + WhatsApp undercount means a "0 ATC, kill it" verdict at R350 spend can be killing an ad whose buyers all went to Sophia, the exact mistake the Andromeda doc warns against in its sequence-learning section, contradicting the Architecture doc's own early-kill rule.

**Action:** Define kill rules on a real sample. CTR/hook-rate and 3-second-video-view can be judged early (those are high-volume signals). Purchase-based kills need ~R1,500-2,500 spend (3-5+ expected conversions) before a verdict, AND must be read at the ad-set/campaign level per the sequence-learning rule, not ad-level. Reconcile this with the Andromeda "never kill a high-CPA ad inside a winning ad set" rule, which the Architecture kill table currently violates. One kill doctrine, not two.

## TIER 3: MARKET, FUNNEL & QUALITY GAPS THE TEAM IS NOT WEIGHING

### 7. SA market saturation: nobody asked if there are 55-120 NEW buyers PER DAY for a dropshipping course in South Africa, indefinitely.
The Spend Math doc honestly flags "97% new customers" and "40 concurrent all-day is a very large store," but it never asks the ceiling question: the SA "start a side hustle / dropshipping" audience is finite, the playbook stays broad-SA-18-45, and you are selling a course (one-time purchase, ~zero repeat), so you must find 55-120 BRAND NEW buyers every single day, forever, from one country's curious-beginner pool. December's 2.1x at R9k/day skimmed the warmest slice. At R20k/day you are 2x+ deeper into a finite national pool. MER compression (which the doc models) is partly fatigue but partly **pool exhaustion**, and pool exhaustion does not recover with fresh creative the way fatigue does. The "no repeat customers" reality means CAC has to be paid back on order ONE, every time, with no LTV tail to lean on.

**Action:** Model audience size, not just creative supply. Estimate the addressable SA reachable pool and your current penetration. Build the LTV tail the course business is missing: the R60 AI Studio upsell, recurring tiers, a continuity/community offer, agency upsell, or a second product to sell the existing buyer base, so you are not 100% dependent on net-new acquisition forever. Plan the geo-expansion or product-expansion lever BEFORE you saturate SA, because the day net-new acquisition stalls, a course business with no repeat revenue has nowhere to go.

### 8. The 97% leak is the biggest lever and it is sequenced "fix first" but the playbook still ladders spend in parallel.
The Landing/CRO section is right that a 3% -> 6% add-to-cart fix halves effective CAC permanently and is worth more than any creative win. But the "order of operations" (fix page -> THEN scale) is contradicted by the Spend Math ladder, which starts climbing spend from Week 0. You cannot both "spend week one fixing the page" and "Week 1: +40% onto the best set." **Decide which doc wins.**

Also: the CRO section makes confident UX claims (delay the DROP10 popup to 12-15s, exit-intent on mobile, sticky bottom bar) with ZERO mention of A/B testing them. At 88% mobile in-app-browser traffic with a popup that is already ~half your orders, moving the popup timing is a high-stakes change that could TANK conversions if wrong. Changing the thing that drives half your revenue on a hunch is the riskiest move in the CRO doc and it is presented as obviously correct.

**Action:** Sequence honestly, fix the page and CAPI in a hard first week at FLAT or reduced spend, then ladder. And do not ship the popup-timing change blind: split-test it (even a simple 50/50 by day or a Shopify A/B app) against the current full-screen popup, measuring blended order rate, before rolling it out. A "fix" that drops popup-driven orders from 50% to 40% wipes out the entire ATC improvement.

### 9. Refund/chargeback -> processor-death spiral is named but the actual fulfilment failure mode is under-weighted, and it compounds with the flag.
The Ops doc correctly links refunds -> chargeback ratio -> processor termination -> same cash death as the Yoco freeze. But it under-weights the specific spiral: at 100 buyers/night concentrated in the 19:00-22:00 window, the single Mac Mini running EVERY AI build is the chokepoint. If AI Studio chokes during the Wednesday-night peak (and you are explicitly UPSELLING speed with the R60 "skip the line"), you get a wave of same-night refund demands from impulse buyers who got nothing, which simultaneously (a) claws back cash you already paid Meta for, (b) pushes your chargeback ratio toward the 1% processor-kill line, and (c) feeds Meta's money-opportunity flag (refund spikes are a documented ban input per the Compliance doc). Three existential systems degrade from ONE Mac Mini falling over on a Wednesday night.

**Action:** The Store-Builder-async + Vercel-AI-Gateway-failover work is correctly identified, make it a HARD GATE before rung 3, not a "right direction." Add a graceful-degradation queue ("your store is building, here's your slot") so a busy Studio never shows a paid buyer an error. Load-test the build path at 5x BEFORE scaling, not during. And honour the R60 speed promise or stop selling it, under-delivering a paid speed upsell is a direct refund driver.

### 10. Dependency on ONE winning angle ("AI builds your store") is a single point of failure dressed up as a strength.
Five of the six sections tell you to weight nearly all new creative on the AI-build angle because it is proven AND safest for the flag. That is correct for survival but creates a brittle monoculture: the angle WILL fatigue at the audience level (not just per-creative, the whole MESSAGE wears out across the SA pool), and your two backup angles (pain/escape-the-9-5 and lifestyle) are the ones the scripts themselves flag as highest ban risk. So your diversification options are the dangerous ones, and your safe option is the one that will saturate.

**Action:** Develop 2-3 NEW compliant angles NOW while the AI-build angle still works, do not wait until it fatigues. Compliant directions the scripts under-explored: the specific PROBLEM ("you tried Shopify and quit at setup"), the SPEED/comparison ("weeks of work vs one evening"), the SKILL/learning frame ("learn how online stores actually work"), local-pride/SA-built. Test them at small budget continuously so you have a proven bench before the hero angle dies, rather than scrambling for a replacement under a fatigued-and-flagged account.

## TIER 4: SMALLER BUT REAL GAPS

### 11. The 40-concurrent goal is honestly debunked in the Spend Math doc, then the rest of the playbook keeps optimizing for spend as if it weren't.
The Spend Math doc does the best work in the whole playbook by showing 40-concurrent-all-day would cost R85-150k/day in pure paid and reframing it as "organic + WhatsApp must carry it." Good. But that conclusion, organic and Sophia must do the heavy lifting, never flows back into the other sections, which are 95% paid mechanics. The organic+paid flywheel is asserted ("organic converts 6x paid") and then ignored as a strategy.

**Action:** If organic carries most of the concurrency and converts 6x, where is the organic content plan? There is none in six sections. Build the organic cadence (the same founder + student-win content fuels both paid AND organic, huge leverage) as a first-class workstream, not a footnote. The 6x organic conversion edge is the cheapest profit in the business and the playbook spends its entire length on the 1x channel.

### 12. The DROP10 / DROP15 discount is treated as free. At ~95% margin it is not, and it interacts with breakeven.
DROP10 is ~half of all orders and the plan leans on it harder at BOF. Every discounted order lowers AOV and therefore RAISES your breakeven ROAS. The breakeven "~1.2x" assumes the R450 AOV, but if half your orders are 10-15% off, your real blended AOV and real breakeven drift. The plan never models the discount into the breakeven math, and it stacks DROP10 (acquisition) + WA15 (recovery) + a returning-visitor DROP15, training the SA market to never pay full price.

**Action:** Put the discount into the breakeven and AOV tables, it is currently invisible. Decide whether DROP10 is a permanent price cut (in which case rename your real prices and stop pretending) or a genuine lever. The returning-visitor escalation is smart only if DROP15 is truly once-off, which the doc flags but which is operationally hard to enforce across cookie-clearing in-app browsers.

### 13. Success metrics are vague. "R10k profit/day" is a stretch-day, not a commitment, and the doc admits it, but never sets the floor the team commits to.
The Spend Math doc honestly says "plan for ~R7k/day sustainable, treat R10k as stretch." That is the most useful line in the playbook, yet the goal everywhere else is stated as R10k/day. The team needs a committed scorecard, not a hero number.

**Action:** Commit to a tiered scorecard: a FLOOR (e.g. hold R5-7k profit/day at R12-15k spend with MER >=1.6x, no account strikes, refund rate <5%, creative output >=15/week), a TARGET, and explicit STOP conditions (MER <1.4x for 3 days -> hold; any Meta strike -> freeze scale; chargeback ratio >0.7% -> slow acquisition; cash buffer <3 days peak spend -> stop laddering). Decisions should be made against the floor and the stop conditions, not the stretch number.

### 14. No competitor / ad-fatigue-at-the-MARKET-level check, and no plan for what the SA dropshipping-course competitors are doing.
You are in a crowded, scammy SA "make money online" space (the CRO doc even notes buyers are cautious "because the space is full of scams"). That means (a) your audience is being hit by competitor ads for the same promise, accelerating message fatigue beyond your own spend, and (b) competitor reports/association could drag your flagged account down. Nothing in the playbook looks at the competitive field.

**Action:** Run a standing Meta Ad Library watch on SA dropshipping-course competitors, to see which angles are saturating the pool, what compliant framings survive (free creative research), and whether anyone is running the income-claim copy that is poisoning reviewer sensitivity for the whole vertical (which raises YOUR flag risk by association).

## THE ONE-LINE SUMMARY FOR THE TEAM
The playbook is excellent at "how to feed Meta" and honest about the flag, the pixel, Yoco and creative supply. What it has NOT solved: a ban-day survival runbook (not just avoidance), a real creative-production system (not just a target), a properly-sized funded cash buffer (not a 3-day estimate), an attribution layer that can actually rank ad sets (blended MER can't), and a resolution to the two places the sections openly contradict each other (ABO-6-adsets vs CBO-1-campaign, and build-a-backup-account vs never-build-one). Fix those five before you touch the budget, and treat R7k/day profit as the floor you commit to, not R10k as the number you chase.
