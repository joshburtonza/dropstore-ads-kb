Create a roughly 10 to 12 minute audio deep-dive with two friendly, confident hosts talking operator to operator, no hype, no salesy energy. The audience is South African e-commerce and dropshipping store owners who run Meta (Facebook and Instagram) ads for their own Shopify stores. The topic is what to do AFTER you launch your ads, because the post-launch discipline matters more than the setup. Explain every piece of jargon in plain language the first time it comes up. Use South African context naturally: rand, VAT, local data costs and device habits, and the warning that overseas case studies may not match local audience behaviour. Do not invent any specific performance numbers or results; any figures mentioned must be clearly illustrative examples, not claims. Make no income or get-rich promises. Cover these points in order with natural back-and-forth: one, leave the campaign alone for the first few days because the system needs many impressions and several days to learn who the buyer is, and editing resets that learning. Two, the pixel gut check in Events Manager, explaining that the pixel is tracking code and Events Manager is the tracking control room, checking that browser events and server events roughly agree within ten to twenty percent, that server events appear at all, and that the purchase count matches Shopify orders. Three, watch leading metrics like cost per add-to-cart and cost per checkout initiated as early signals instead of obsessing over ROAS, which is return on ad spend and a slow lagging number. Four, judge the whole account not individual ads, and accept an early learning tax while finding winners. Five, use breakdowns by age, gender, placement, platform and audience to confirm spend is hitting the right people. Six, pause far less than instinct says, only killing an ad after it has spent about twice the target cost per purchase with no sales, and remember a big spender can quietly support the whole account. Seven, iterate horizontal first with many different concepts, then vertical with many variations of proven winners. Eight, study long-running competitor ads in the free Meta Ads Library to find proven templates and adapt them. End firmly on the concrete weekly routine: one upload day for new creatives, one deep-dive analysis day for decisions, and watch-and-wait with no touching on every other day, and tell the listener to open a calendar now and write in their upload day and deep-dive day for this week.
