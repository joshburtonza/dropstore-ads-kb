# Your Ad Dashboard Is Lying to You: A Free Taste of DropStore Deep Dives

Quick one, and it might be the most useful few minutes of your week. We are going to give you something you can use today, no strings, and then show you where the rest of this lives.

Here is the free value. Most store owners judge their ads by one number: the ROAS shown inside the ads manager. You spend a thousand rand, it says three times return, so you assume you made three thousand. The problem is that this number quietly stops being true at the exact moment it matters most, which is when you start to scale.

Why? Because that number is built from what the tracking pixel can see, and the pixel is just a piece of code trying to connect a sale back to an ad. Real buyers do not behave the way it assumes. They click an ad on their phone, get distracted, come back that night on a laptop, ask a question on WhatsApp, then buy two days later. Every one of those hops breaks the trail. So the platform credits some sales to the wrong ad, misses others completely, and double counts a few. On a small budget the error is small. As you scale, the gap gets wide, and you end up making big-money decisions off a number that is half fiction.

So what do the smart stores use instead? One simple number called blended MER, which just means marketing efficiency ratio. Take all the revenue your store actually made this week, from your own back office, not the ad platform. Divide it by everything you spent on ads in the same week. That ratio is the truth. It does not care about cookies or browsers or attribution windows. It answers the only question that pays your bills: for every rand I put in, how many came back? Make that your scoreboard and you will stop scaling losers that looked like winners on the dashboard.

Here is a second one to go with it, because the two work together. Before you judge any return number, you need to know your breakeven. That is the point where you make exactly zero profit. Work out what you actually keep on an average order after product cost, shipping, payment fees, and VAT. Say you keep forty five cents of every rand. One divided by that is about two point two. So you need a blended MER of roughly two point two just to stand still. A two point zero that looked fine on the dashboard is quietly bleeding you. Once you know your breakeven, every spending decision gets simple.
